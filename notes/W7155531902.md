# Dynamic Parameterization of Priestley-Taylor Coefficient for a More Accurate Potential Evapotranspiration Estimation in Ungauged Regions

**Authors:** Pushpendra Raghav, Mukesh Kumar
**Venue:** Environmental Research Letters · **Date:** 2026-04-24 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1088/1748-9326/ae6464
**Score:** 9/10<sup> top pick</sup>
**Source:** abstract

## TL;DR

Fixed α_PT = 1.26 in Priestley-Taylor PET is replaced by biome- and season-specific functional equations derived from 3,128 site-years of FluxNet data, improving PET, ET, and drought-stress estimates globally.

## Summary

- **Problem:** The Priestley-Taylor PET method uses a fixed coefficient (α_PT = 1.26) regardless of biome or season, ignoring aerodynamic and plant-conductance constraints that cause systematic errors across ecosystems and in ungauged regions.
- **Method:** Optimized α_PT across 246 FluxNet sites worldwide (3,128 site-years of eddy covariance data) spanning diverse biomes; identified key drivers via regression; derived simplified functional equations linking α_PT to temperature and radiation for direct use in land-surface and hydrologic models.
- **Key result:** α_PT shows strong spatial and seasonal variability — higher in forests and winter, lower in savanna summers. Functional-equation-derived PET yielded improved estimates of the Evaporative Stress Index (ESI) and actual ET compared to the fixed-coefficient baseline; specific RMSE/bias numbers not reported in abstract.
- **Why it matters:** Drop-in replacement equations for α_PT can be incorporated into existing land-surface models and remote-sensing PET products without additional data requirements, directly improving drought assessment and water-stress estimation in ungauged basins.
- **Caveats:** Performance validation is largely site-based (FluxNet); how well functional equations transfer to data-sparse regions with distinct vegetation or soil types beyond the FluxNet network is unclear. Quantitative improvement metrics not detailed in the abstract.
