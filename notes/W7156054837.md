# Mapping PFAS Exceedance Risk in China's Surface Water: A Machine Learning Approach Informed by Source Distribution

**Authors:** Yì Wáng, Shuai Shao, Qiufeng Gao et al.
**Venue:** Environmental Science & Technology · **Date:** 2026-04-27 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1021/acs.est.6c00574
**Score:** 9/10 <sup>top pick</sup>
**Source:** abstract

## TL;DR

A geographically weighted random forest model maps PFAS exceedance risk across China's surface water at 1 km resolution, identifying 80–90 million people in high-risk zones.

## Summary

- **Problem:** Nationwide PFAS distribution in China's surface water is poorly characterized due to sparse monitoring, despite China being the world's leading fluorochemical producer and consumer.
- **Method:** Geographically Weighted Random Forest (GWR-RF) integrating a spatially explicit inventory of >280,000 potential PFAS source locations; trained to predict exceedance risk; validated with accuracy = 0.83, ROC-AUC = 0.91; output at 1 km resolution.
- **Key result:** Hotspots concentrated in the eastern coastal plain and key inland industrial provinces; 80–90 million people reside in high-risk areas. Proximity to known PFAS users and annual precipitation are the dominant predictors of elevated risk.
- **Why it matters:** Provides the first high-resolution national exceedance-risk map for PFAS in Chinese surface water, offering a spatial evidence base for targeted regulatory action and monitoring prioritization.
- **Caveats:** Risk map reflects exceedance probability, not measured concentrations; source inventory completeness and monitoring data sparsity limit validation; causal inference on precipitation driver requires further mechanistic study.
