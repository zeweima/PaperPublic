# A Numerical Study of the Effects of Density Contrast on Flow, Turbulence, and Mixing at the Negro/Solimoes Confluence, Brazil

**Authors:** C. Jiang, M. Koken, G. Constantinescu et al.
**Venue:** Water Resources Research · **Date:** 2026-04-28 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1029/2025wr041934
**Score:** 9/10<sup> top pick</sup>
**Source:** full text

## TL;DR

At the world's largest-discharge confluence (>140,000 m³/s, W/D > 100), even a small density contrast (Δρ = 0.36 kg/m³, Ri = 0.046) reverses mixing-interface tilt direction and reorganizes secondary circulation cells.

## Summary

- **Problem:** How density contrast between confluent rivers controls mixing-interface (MI) geometry, secondary flow, and turbulent mixing at a large-discharge, large-aspect-ratio confluence — unknown from field data alone due to extreme sampling conditions.
- **Method:** Detached eddy simulations (DES, ~0.7 million cells) of the Negro/Solimões confluence near Manaus, Brazil (Q_total ≈ 140,000 m³/s, W/D > 100, junction angle ≈ 70°) under three scenarios: base real conditions (denser fluid in faster Solimões tributary, Ri = 0.046, Fr_ρ = 4.66), no density difference (Ri = 0), and reversed density (denser fluid in slower Negro tributary). Validated against ADCP field data from two surveys (October 2014, April/May 2015).
- **Key result:** Despite Δρ = 0.36 kg/m³ and very large discharges, the near-bed intrusion of heavier Solimões fluid is well-developed by section P5 and reverses the MI tilt relative to the no-density case. In the no-density case, bathymetric steering alone tilts the MI strongly toward the Negro bank; real density contrast (Ri = 0.046) is sufficient to overcome this and reverse tilt direction. Secondary-cell widths in the real case are 10.8D and 16.4D (left/right), versus 17.6D and 21.2D in the reversed-density case. No streamwise-oriented vortical (SOV) cells form in any scenario despite the large confluence angle, consistent with high W/D suppressing them.
- **Why it matters:** First eddy-resolving simulation of flow and mixing at a large-discharge, large-aspect-ratio confluence; shows that density contrast effects (Fr_ρ ≤ 5) dominate MI geometry even when topographic steering is strong, with implications for contaminant transport, sediment routing, and ecological habitat delineation at Amazon-scale junctions.
- **Caveats:** Single high-flow condition simulated; bed sediment transport not coupled; molecular Schmidt number set to 6 (not field-representative for temperature); generalization to other large confluences with different planform geometry requires further study.
