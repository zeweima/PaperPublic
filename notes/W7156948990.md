# Climate change and irrigation expansion reshape the water pressure and upstream–downstream interactions in the Lancang–Mekong River Basin

**Authors:** Hongling Zhao, Zilong Zhao, Khosro Morovati et al.
**Venue:** HESS (Hydrology and Earth System Sciences) · **Date:** 2026-04-28 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.5194/hess-30-2433-2026
**Score:** 9/10<sup> top pick</sup>
**Source:** full text

## TL;DR

Under SSP5–8.5, irrigation withdrawal in the Lancang–Mekong reaches 19% of annual and 59% of dry-season available water by 2040, with upstream China-driven external pressure increasingly dominating midstream subregions.

## Summary

- **Problem:** Transboundary irrigation water stress in the Lancang–Mekong Basin stems from internal local withdrawal, external upstream appropriation, and climate change; their relative contributions across 13 subregions and over time are unquantified.
- **Method:** Pressure–State–Response (PSR) framework applied basin-wide (1980–2040); THREW hydrological model calibrated at 15 stations (NSE > 0.76 at all sites); CMIP6 5-GCM ensemble under SSP1–2.6, SSP2–4.5, SSP5–8.5; remote-sensing-derived irrigation canal network at 0.5 m resolution; FAO/SPAM irrigated-area data 1980–2024. Two reservoir scenarios (pre-dam / post-dam) isolate hydraulic-infrastructure effects.
- **Key result:** Irrigation withdrawal/available-water ratio rose from ~8% annually and ~27% dry-season (1980–2020 mean) to projected 19%/59% (SSP5–8.5, 2040). Downstream subregions (9–13) experience 3–4% annual and 10–15% dry-season reductions in available water from upstream withdrawals. Subregion 8 (Thailand) flips from internally dominated to externally dominated pressure under all future scenarios. 10 of 13 subregions show simultaneous increases in internal and external pressure by 2001–2020 vs. 1980–2000. Subregion 13 (Vietnam delta) is the highest absolute-pressure hotspot (internal ~23%, external ~18% of available water under future multi-year means).
- **Why it matters:** Differentiates governance responsibility by subregion — upstream (China, Subregion 1) shows low-efficiency high-internal-pressure, mid-stream (Subregions 4–5) faces intensifying external competition, downstream faces compound pressure redistribution. The 8-type irrigation pressure classification system supports targeted transboundary water allocation negotiations.
- **Caveats:** Projections cover only 2021–2040 (near-term); irrigated-area expansion follows MRC development plans which may diverge from actual policy; groundwater irrigation not explicitly modelled; inter-model spread increases under dry-season SSP scenarios (±3–5% SD).
