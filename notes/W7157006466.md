# PULSE: A Novel Potential Underlying Water Use Efficiency‐Based Method for Latent Heat and Surface Energy Imbalance Correction

**Authors:** Pushpendra Raghav, Mukesh Kumar
**Venue:** Water Resources Research · **Date:** 2026-04-28 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1029/2025wr042766
**Score:** 8/10<sup> top pick</sup>
**Source:** full text

## TL;DR

PULSE corrects eddy covariance ET for energy imbalance using plant physiological water-use efficiency constraints, outperforming Bowen-ratio and available-energy closure methods across 257 global flux sites.

## Summary

- **Problem:** EC flux measurements persistently underclose the surface energy budget (EBR deficits of 10–30% are common), biasing ET estimates used in hydrological budgets, land-surface model benchmarking, and ESM calibration. Existing corrections (OFC Bowen-ratio, AEC available-energy) impose full closure assumptions that are physically unjustified and can distort H and LE partitioning.
- **Method:** PULSE exploits the principle that potential underlying water-use efficiency (uWUE_p = GPP·√VPD/ET) is intra-annually stable for a given vegetation type. The method uses 95th-percentile quantile regression of GPP·√VPD vs. ET to estimate uWUE_p across energy-balance-ratio (EBR) bins; LOESS smoothing relates uWUE_p to EBR. ET is upward-corrected only when uWUE_p is anomalously elevated at low EBR, indicating systematic ET underestimation. Applied to >250 FLUXNET/ICOS/OzFlux/JapanFlux sites (2775 site-years); evaluated against OFC and AEC with an ANN generalization test and an ecosystem stomatal conductance model.
- **Key result:** ANN models trained on PULSE-corrected LE achieved the highest R² and lowest normalized RMSE vs. OFC- and AEC-corrected LE across all plant functional types, in both held-out test data and periods of independent good energy-balance closure (0.9 ≤ EBR ≤ 1.1). Maximum stomatal conductance (g_sc,max) derived from PULSE-corrected LE had the lowest coefficient of variation across EBR bins — more stable than OFC, AEC, or uncorrected LE — indicating greater physiological consistency. The ecosystem conductance model explained on average 36–41% of sub-daily conductance variability, with some sites reaching R² = 95%.
- **Why it matters:** PULSE selectively corrects LE without enforcing full energy closure or assuming Bowen-ratio constancy, preserving natural temporal dynamics. Corrected fluxes provide a more reliable benchmark for ESM evaluation, carbon cycle studies, and hydrological water-budget closure; method requires only flux-tower GPP and LE, broadly deployable.
- **Caveats:** GPP bias propagates directly into uWUE_p and correction magnitude. Assumption of stable uWUE_p weakens in evaporation-dominated systems (wetlands, flooded crops) where ET–GPP coupling is loose. Intra-annual uWUE_p divergence from disturbance, stress, or land-cover transitions can introduce errors. H is not explicitly corrected (only LE), though H_corr can be inferred residually.
