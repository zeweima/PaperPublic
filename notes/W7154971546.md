# Trade-offs and synergies of conservation agriculture practices in the maize–wheat system: Improving yield, profitability, and soil resilience in a changing climate

**Authors:** Mohammad Hasanain, V.K. Singh, S.S. Rathore et al.
**Venue:** Field Crops Research · **Date:** 2026-04-10 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.fcr.2026.110486
**Score:** n/a/10
**Source:** full text

## TL;DR

Permanent raised beds + residue retention + GreenSeeker-guided N application raised maize-wheat system yield 13%, net returns ~27%, and soil quality index 6–9% over conventional tillage in semi-arid India.

## Summary

- **Problem:** South Asian maize-wheat systems face stagnating yields, declining SOC, and poor resource-use efficiency under conventional intensive tillage; combined effects of permanent raised beds (PRB), residue management, and site-specific nutrient management (SSNM) on productivity, profitability, and soil health are not well quantified.
- **Method:** Two-year split-split-plot field experiment (2018–19, 2019–20) at ICAR-IARI, New Delhi (semi-arid); factors: tillage (conventional tillage vs. PRB), residue (removal –R vs. retention +R at 3 Mg ha⁻¹), and three nutrient strategies (soil-test based STB; Nutrient Expert NE; NE + GreenSeeker NE+GS). Maize equivalent yield (MEY), economics, soil bulk density, SOC, microbial biomass C, dehydrogenase activity, soil quality index (SQI), and GHG eco-efficiency assessed.
- **Key result:** PRB increased MEY 13% over CT and reduced bulk density. Residue retention (+R) further raised MEY 2.4–5.5% and increased SOC retention 10.81% vs. –R. NE+GS achieved highest MEY (5.56–5.65 Mg ha⁻¹), profitability (+20.3% REE over NMO2-NE), and energy-use efficiency. Net returns under PRB were ~27% higher than CT. SQI improved 6% under PRB and 3.8–9.3% under NE+GS. SOC sequestration rate (SOCR) was highest under PRB+residue+NE+GS (0.697 Mg ha⁻¹ yr⁻¹), 48.6% above CT+residue removal baseline. System cost of cultivation was 18% lower under PRB than CT.
- **Why it matters:** Demonstrates that combining PRB, residue retention, and optical-sensor-guided N management in a single system delivers simultaneous yield, profit, and soil-health co-benefits with minimal trade-offs; provides evidence base for scaling CA interventions across the Indo-Gangetic Plains to meet SDG2/13/15 targets.
- **Caveats:** Only two cropping years at one semi-arid site; higher operational costs and labor for residue retention may constrain smallholder adoption; long-term GHG accounting beyond CO₂-equivalent eco-efficiency not fully quantified.
