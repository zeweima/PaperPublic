# High-resolution land surface modeling of climate and CO₂ effects on ecosystem carbon-water coupling across the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau

**Authors:** Xiazhen Xi, Xing Yuan, Yi Hao
**Venue:** Agricultural and Forest Meteorology · **Date:** 2026-04-21 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.agrformet.2026.111195
**Score:** 8/10
**Source:** full text

## TL;DR

Elevated CO₂ dominates WUE increase on the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau (116% of the trend), while warming-driven respiration suppresses NEWUE in eastern sub-regions.

## Summary

- **Problem:** Dominant drivers of water use efficiency (WUE) and net ecosystem WUE (NEWUE) trends on the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau (QTP) are uncertain because global LSMs lack QTP-specific process representation and carbon-water coupling for alpine ecosystems with freeze-thaw dynamics.
- **Method:** Coupled a vegetation carbon allocation and soil carbon cycling module to the high-resolution Conjunctive Surface-Subsurface Process v2 LSM (CSSPv2-Carbon); ran 1982–2020 simulations at 6 km resolution (453 × 316 grid cells); five factorial sensitivity experiments isolated temperature, precipitation, energy, and CO₂ effects; validated against 11 ChinaFLUX/CERN carbon flux stations and 3 hydrological stations; benchmarked against CLM5-BGC and Noah-MP.
- **Key result:** CSSPv2-Carbon improved GPP correlation 13% and reduced NEP RMSE 48% vs. CLM5-BGC. Over 1982–2020, plateau-wide WUE increased significantly while NEWUE decreased in some sub-regions. Elevated CO₂ contributed 116% of the WUE trend and 187% of the NEWUE trend by boosting GPP while suppressing ET via stomatal closure. Precipitation contributed 33% to NEWUE enhancement; temperature and radiation synergy exerted a combined −87% inhibitory effect on WUE trends, exceeding individual driver effects.
- **Why it matters:** CO₂ fertilization, not warming-wetting, is the primary driver of carbon-water coupling change on the QTP; misattributing this in global LSMs distorts projections of water yield and carbon sequestration in a region feeding major Asian rivers.
- **Caveats:** HR is systematically underestimated by all three models due to poor simulation of freeze-thaw soil microbial dynamics. NEE-derived GPP/ER partitioning relies on nighttime respiratory extrapolation assumptions. Results are sensitive to VMAX25 and microbial respiration parameterization, tuned specifically for QTP vegetation types.
