# [Characterizing iron-bound organic carbon in cold seep sediment cores: impacts of anaerobic oxidation of methane](https://doi.org/10.1007/s10533-026-01334-y)

**Authors:** Yang Wu, Qianyong Liang, Huachun Liu et al.
**Venue:** Biogeochemistry · **Date:** 2026-04-17 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1007/s10533-026-01334-y
**Score:** 9/10<sup> top pick</sup>
**Source:** abstract

## TL;DR

In cold seep sediments, AOM simultaneously erodes and enriches iron-bound organic carbon pools, with methane-derived OC preferentially binding reactive iron oxides.

## Summary

- **Problem:** The "rusty sink" mechanism—reactive iron oxide (FeR) preservation of organic carbon—is well characterized in estuaries but poorly constrained in deep-sea methane-rich environments where anaerobic oxidation of methane (AOM) actively reshapes iron and carbon chemistry.
- **Method:** Sediment cores from the Qiongdongnan Basin, northern South China Sea; measured reactive iron-bound OC (FeR-OC), bulk OC, δ¹³C of FeR-OC vs. bulk TOC, and methane-derived OC fractions across sulfate-methane transition zones (SMTZs) and non-SMTZ layers.
- **Key result:** FeR-OC contributed 5.2–40.0% of total OC (mean 15.7 ± 8.1%), declining toward methane-rich zones as iron oxide sulfidization and pyrite formation consume reactive Fe. Methane-derived OC accounted for 0–11.1% of TOC and 0–12.7% of FeR-OC, with higher fractions in SMTZs; δ¹³C-FeR-OC was more depleted than δ¹³C-TOC, confirming preferential binding of methane-derived OC to reactive iron.
- **Why it matters:** Reveals dual competing processes in deep-sea carbon sequestration: sulfidization destroys FeR-OC while sulfate-dependent AOM generates methane-derived OC that partially offsets the loss. Bulk Fe content is not the primary control in methane-rich settings—OC source and FeR-OC/FeR ratios are. This reframes how iron–carbon coupling should be parameterized in deep-sea biogeochemical models.
- **Caveats:** Single basin (South China Sea); limited replication across contrasting seep systems and methane flux regimes; long-term preservation potential of AOM-derived FeR-OC not directly tested.
