# Repeated snowball–hothouse cycles within the Neoproterozoic Sturtian glaciation

**Authors:** Charlotte Minsky, Robin Wordsworth, David T. Johnston et al.
**Venue:** PNAS · **Date:** 2026-04-27 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.2525919123
**Score:** 8/10 top pick
**Source:** abstract

## TL;DR

Franklin large igneous province weathering drove Earth into a limit cycle alternating Snowball and hothouse states, resolving the ~56 Myr Sturtian glaciation duration problem.

## Summary

- **Problem:** Canonical Snowball and Slushball models cannot account for the Sturtian glaciation lasting ~56 Myr; no mechanistic explanation exists for its anomalous duration.
- **Method:** Coupled box model of Neoproterozoic climate and carbon cycle; weathering flux from the Franklin igneous province used as the key forcing parameter; tested against sedimentation records and oxygen proxy data.
- **Key result:** Franklin province weathering pushes the climate system into a limit cycle regime alternating between full Snowball and hothouse states, matching the observed ~56 Myr duration; consistent with known sedimentary patterns and predicts stable syn-Sturtian atmospheric oxygen.
- **Why it matters:** Provides a self-consistent dynamical mechanism for Earth's longest known glaciation, with implications for how large igneous provinces regulate deep-time climate oscillations and how the biosphere was buffered during extreme glacial episodes.
- **Caveats:** Box model simplicity may miss spatial heterogeneity; oxygen stability predictions lack direct geochemical validation in this study; Franklin province weathering rates are uncertain.
