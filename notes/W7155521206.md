# The effects of a constructed closure of the Bering Strait on AMOC tipping behavior

**Authors:** Jelle Soons, Henk A. Dijkstra
**Venue:** Science Advances · **Date:** 2026-04-24 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1126/sciadv.aeb7887
**Score:** 9/10 <sup>top pick</sup>
**Source:** full text

## TL;DR

A Bering Strait Dam extends the AMOC's safe CO₂ budget by up to ~500 PgC if deployed while the AMOC is still sufficiently strong.

## Summary

- **Problem:** AMOC collapse under CO₂ and freshwater forcing is a major climate tipping risk; whether artificially closing the Bering Strait (a "Bering Strait Dam," BSD) can prevent collapse under realistic climate forcing is unknown.
- **Method:** Earth system Model of Intermediate Complexity (CLIMBER-X, 5°×5° resolution); hysteresis experiments with freshwater hosing (0–0.35 sverdrup), 1%/year CO₂ forcing runs to 1500 years, and closure-delay experiments (0–300 years after forcing onset); safe carbon budget defined as maximum cumulative emissions before AMOC collapse (increments of 100 PgC).
- **Key result:** Under a closed Bering Strait (CBS), the AMOC's critical hosing threshold rises from 0.220 to 0.195 sverdrup, AMOC strength at 26°N increases by ~0.3 sverdrup, and the safe carbon budget is extended by up to ~500 PgC relative to an open strait — but only when background freshwater flux is low (F_H ≤ ~0.075 sverdrup). For higher hosing (weaker AMOC), closure reduces the budget. A timely closure has an operating window of at least 150 years after 1%/year CO₂ forcing commences; closures at or after ~250 years accelerate collapse relative to open-strait conditions. Critical AMOC strength below which closure fails: 9.4 ± 1.3 sverdrup (scenario 1), 7.8 ± 0.3 sverdrup (scenario 2), 9.8 ± 1.0 sverdrup (scenario 3).
- **Why it matters:** Demonstrates a physically plausible geoengineering intervention — a ~80 km dam at 50 m average depth, comparable in scale to completed megaprojects — that can extend the AMOC's safe operating space without CO₂ removal; identifies the critical AMOC strength and timing constraints for effectiveness.
- **Caveats:** CLIMBER-X runs at coarse 5°×5° resolution and treats Bering Strait throughflow as baroclinic tracer exchange only (not barotropic), underestimating throughflow by ~1 order of magnitude vs. observations; CO₂ forcing is stylized (1%/year); safe budget range (~500 PgC) is small relative to critical thresholds (~5000 PgC), making quantitative estimates model-dependent; regional ecosystem, economic, and governance impacts of the BSD not assessed.
