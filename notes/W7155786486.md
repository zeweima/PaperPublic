# Winter North Atlantic tripole sea surface temperature drives following summer synchronous heat waves over Eurasia since the 2000s

**Authors:** Yan Chen, Wen Chen, Shangfeng Chen et al.
**Venue:** Environmental Research Letters · **Date:** 2026-04-27 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1088/1748-9326/ae6520
**Score:** 8/10 top pick
**Source:** abstract

## TL;DR

Winter North Atlantic tripole SST anomalies persist into summer and trigger an eastward wave train that produces synchronous heat waves in Eastern Europe and East Asia.

## Summary

- **Problem:** Synchronous extreme heat events co-occurring across multiple Eurasian regions have larger impacts on agriculture and water resources than localized events, but their shared drivers are poorly understood.
- **Method:** Statistical analysis of observed SST anomalies (North Atlantic tripole pattern) and summer extreme high temperature events over Eastern Europe and East Asia; wave-train propagation diagnostics; focus on post-2000s period when NAT persistence has strengthened.
- **Key result:** Winter North Atlantic tripole SST anomalies persist via air-sea interaction into the following summer, exciting an eastward-propagating atmospheric wave train that generates anticyclonic anomalies over both Eastern Europe and East Asia, suppressing cloud cover, enhancing shortwave radiation, and producing synchronous extreme heat. Post-2000s, winter-to-summer persistence of the NAT pattern has increased, strengthening this teleconnection.
- **Why it matters:** Identifies a seasonal precursor signal (winter NAT SST) that could be exploited for multi-month lead prediction of co-occurring Eurasian heat waves, with implications for early warnings affecting agriculture, water supply, and ecosystems across two continents simultaneously.
- **Caveats:** Mechanism established statistically over a relatively short post-2000s period; causal role of NAT versus confounding climate modes (AMO, ENSO) not fully disentangled; physical persistence mechanism deserves model confirmation.
