# Impact of vertical coordinate systems on simulations of barotropic and baroclinic tides in the Yellow Sea using a regional MOM6 configuration for the Northwest Pacific

**Authors:** Inseong Chang, Young Ho Kim, Young-Gyu Park et al.
**Venue:** Geoscientific Model Development · **Date:** 2026-04-21 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.5194/gmd-19-3053-2026
**Score:** 9/10
**Source:** full text

## TL;DR

Hybrid isopycnal coordinates (HYBRID) outperform z* (ZSTAR) for both barotropic and baroclinic tides in the Yellow Sea, primarily by suppressing spurious diapycnal mixing that degrades internal-tide propagation in ZSTAR.

## Summary

- **Problem:** No systematic evaluation existed of how vertical coordinate choice affects barotropic vs. baroclinic tidal simulation in a realistically forced shelf-sea model where energetic tides and strong seasonal stratification co-occur.
- **Method:** Regional MOM6 at 1/24° covering the Northwest Pacific (99–170° E, 5–63° N); two 75-layer configurations — ZSTAR (z*) and HYBRID (z*-isopycnal) — run over 20 years (10-yr spin-up + 10-yr hindcast); 10 tidal constituents from TPXO9; validated against OSTIA SST, KODC in situ temperature profiles, and TPXO9 tidal harmonics; baroclinic energy budget diagnostics on final year.
- **Key result:** HYBRID M₂ RMSE = 24.19 cm (correlation 0.76) vs. ZSTAR RMSE = 32.55 cm (correlation 0.67); K₁ RMSE 4.72 cm vs. 5.70 cm. HYBRID barotropic energy fluxes 13.5%–42.3% larger than ZSTAR in winter and 17.2%–51.1% larger in summer across four key tidal regions. In summer, HYBRID baroclinic KE is 9.2%–33.6% greater; in winter, ZSTAR shows 19.5%–23.6% more baroclinic KE, attributed to spurious numerical conversion rather than physical internal tides. HYBRID SST RMSE = 0.58 °C vs. 0.75 °C for ZSTAR; ZSTAR thermocline bias exceeds −4 °C locally in summer.
- **Why it matters:** Spurious diapycnal mixing in ZSTAR locally dissipates converted baroclinic energy rather than radiating it, degrading internal-tide propagation and vertical energy pathways — a systematic error relevant to any coastal/shelf model used for mixing, biogeochemistry, or sea-level prediction in tidally energetic, seasonally stratified seas.
- **Caveats:** Single Yellow Sea domain; salinity validation excluded due to known data quality issues; Yangtze freshwater stratification effect in HYBRID not directly validated against observations; no wetting/drying scheme limits applicability in intertidal zones.
