# [42 Years of straw incorporation drive macroaggregation, enhancing macropores and POM in a Vertisol: Insights from X-ray μCT](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.geoderma.2026.117818)

**Authors:** Zichun Guo, Tianyu Ding, Zizhou Yu et al.
**Venue:** Geoderma · **Date:** 2026 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.geoderma.2026.117818
**Score:** <unclear from abstract>/10
**Source:** full text

## TL;DR

42 years of straw + chemical fertilization in a Vertisol increased 3–5 mm macroaggregates, surface-connected macroporosity by 230%, and intra-aggregate POM volume by 281%, driven by enhanced GRSP, SOC, and MBC.

## Summary

- **Problem:** How long-term straw incorporation jointly affects aggregate stability, intra-aggregate pore architecture, and the spatial distribution of fresh vs. decomposed POM within macroaggregates in Vertisols is poorly resolved.
- **Method:** 42-year field experiment (Shajiang black soil, Vertisol, China) with four treatments — Control, NPK, NPK + half straw (NPKLS), NPK + full straw (NPKHS). X-ray μCT at 6 μm voxel resolution on intact 3–5 mm aggregates (n = 20 per treatment); machine-learning (random forest, 93.4% accuracy) segmentation of macropores, fresh POM (FPOM), and decomposed POM (DPOM); PLS-PM path modeling for causal inference.
- **Key result:** Versus Control, NPKHS increased GRSP by 81.6%, SOC by 51.7%, MBC by 131%; proportion of 3–5 mm aggregates and MWD rose significantly. Surface-connected porosity increased 230%, isolated porosity 166%, connection probability 94.6% (all P < 0.05). Fresh POM volume density within aggregates rose 281% and decomposed POM volume density rose 93.9% under NPKHS. PLS-PM: C input → binding agents (β = 0.97) → aggregation (β = 0.91) → pore structure (β = 0.83); direct POM → pore structure path was non-significant (β = 0.32).
- **Why it matters:** Demonstrates that the macroaggregation pathway — not direct POM–pore interaction — controls pore network development; full straw return is more effective than half-rate for improving Vertisol physical structure and C sequestration. CT + ML classification of FPOM vs. DPOM provides in situ spatial resolution unavailable from density fractionation.
- **Caveats:** Single site (one Vertisol, subhumid monsoon climate); CT limited to 3–5 mm aggregates; only five aggregates scanned per treatment due to cost; soil acidification under mineral fertilization confounds interpretation of NPK-only effects.
