# [SWOT Satellite Observations of the Kakhovka Dam Break Flood Highlight Limitations of Outburst Flood Models](https://doi.org/10.1029/2025GL120832)

**Authors:** K. E. Lehnigk, T. M. Pavelsky, K. A. Lang
**Venue:** Geophysical Research Letters · **Date:** 2026-04-02 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1029/2025GL120832

**Source:** full text

## TL;DR

SWOT's first 2D time-series of a large outburst flood shows that even best-available bathymetry leaves 2D hydraulic models unable to reproduce both peak stage and flood timing simultaneously.

## Summary

- **Problem:** Outburst floods are nearly impossible to validate at scale because in-situ measurements are sparse; it is unclear whether 2D shallow-wave hydraulic models can reliably predict flood stage and timing for geohazard assessment.
- **Method:** SWOT water surface elevations (~10 cm accuracy) collected daily along ~125 km of the Dnipro River downstream of the Kakhovka Dam (breached 6 June 2023) were compared against GeoClaw 2D hydraulic simulations using three reservoir/channel bathymetry configurations: GLOBathy lake-style, river-like interpolated (700 m historical channel, −4 m minimum elevation), and river-like with steep channel (slope 3×10⁻⁵ m/m). ICESat-2 ATL08 elevations of the exposed reservoir bed (August 2023) independently validated bathymetric accuracy.
- **Key result:** GLOBathy bathymetry underpredicted peak stage by 6.1 m on average and overpredicted volume (48 km³ modeled vs. 19 km³ actual), with mean stage error of −97% (June 6–9) flipping to +71% (June 10–23). The best configuration (river-like reservoir + steep channel) reduced peak stage error to 1.4 m underprediction but still overestimated peak arrival time by only 3 hr while all models projected flood recession 810–2730 hr slower than SWOT's observed 350–375 hr return to pre-flood levels. No single bathymetry reproduced both flood magnitude and timing. ICESat-2 confirmed the river-like bathymetry was more accurate (median offset 6.10 m for GLOBathy vs. 6.10 m — GLOBathy median offset 8.64 m vs. river-like <2 m in the critical near-dam zone).
- **Why it matters:** Establishes the first direct, spatially continuous 2D observational benchmark for a large dam-break outburst flood; demonstrates that current 2D shallow-wave models are insufficient for reliable geohazard decision-making even when bathymetry is improved, and highlights SWOT (and future wide-swath SAR) as essential validation infrastructure for outburst flood modeling globally.
- **Caveats:** Single event (Kakhovka, Ukraine); geopolitical context limited in-situ data; SWOT vector product quality degraded within 4 km of dam and during some overpass days; model sensitivity to surface roughness and outlet size tested but secondary to bathymetry; 3D modeling or revised shallow-wave parameterizations not evaluated.
