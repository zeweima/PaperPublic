# [Exploring controls on solute export mechanisms in anthropogenically impacted catchments in Southern Germany in climatically different periods](https://doi.org/10.5194/hess-30-2059-2026)

**Authors:** Sofia Frietsch, Tobias Schuetz
**Venue:** Hydrology and Earth System Sciences · **Date:** 2026-04-15 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.5194/hess-30-2059-2026
**Score:** <unclear from abstract>/10
**Source:** full text

## TL;DR

Warmer, drier recent decades shifted nutrient export in 40 southern German catchments from dilution toward enrichment, with geogenic solutes largely unaffected.

## Summary

- **Problem:** How climate change alters solute export mechanisms (SEM) — the concentration–discharge (c–Q) relationship — for nutrients and geogenic minerals across anthropogenically impacted catchments; long-term multi-solute evidence across diverse catchments was lacking.
- **Method:** Monthly/biweekly c–Q time series (8–20 years) for NO₃-N, NH₄-N, SRP, TP, TOC, Ca²⁺, and Mg²⁺ across 40 catchments in southern Germany. Two climatically distinct periods compared: Period 1 (before Jan 2012, cooler/wetter) vs. Period 2 (since Jan 2012, warmer/drier; mean annual air temperature increased 8.68 → 9.70 °C, actual ET 460 → 490 mm, precipitation declined 905 → 819 mm). SEM quantified via power-law c–Q slope b and CV_C/CV_Q ratio; ANCOVA tested period differences.
- **Key result:** Nutrients (NH₄-N, SRP, TP, TOC) shifted toward enrichment in Period 2 (positive Δb), most pronounced in catchments previously showing dilution. SRP dilution weakened significantly across 32 catchments (p < 0.01). NO₃-N showed slight strengthened enrichment but no statistically significant period effect; geogenic solutes (Ca²⁺, Mg²⁺) exhibited only slight, insignificant increase in dilution. Declining NH₄-N concentrations in 57.5% of catchments and NO₃-N in 72.5% reflect legacy fertilizer reduction since 1990, yet export dynamics shifted enrichment-ward. Evapotranspiration emerged as the dominant climatic control amplifying enrichment for SRP, TP, and TOC in Period 2; soil moisture and drought index increasingly regulated NH₄-N export.
- **Why it matters:** Enrichment-driven SEM under warming means peak solute concentrations will more frequently exceed regulatory water-quality benchmarks, requiring updated catchment management strategies even as mean concentrations decline.
- **Caveats:** Monthly/biweekly sampling likely underestimates event-driven peak loads; the Period 1/2 split at 2012 is arbitrary and reflects a gradual trend, not a step change; results may be biased toward mid-mountain catchments with specific geology and land use.
