# First global carbon dynamics from an observational and process-informed hybrid perspective: Oversimplified respiration representation likely drives divergence in terrestrial carbon sequestration across models

**Authors:** Songyan Zhu, Wenquan Dong, Vasileios Myrgiotis et al.
**Venue:** Agricultural and Forest Meteorology · **Date:** 2026-04-27 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.agrformet.2026.111197
**Score:** 9/10 <sup>top pick</sup>
**Source:** full text

## TL;DR

A process-informed ML hybrid (UFLUX) pins the global terrestrial carbon sink at ~16 PgC yr⁻¹, splitting the difference between process models (6 PgC yr⁻¹) and pure upscaling (26 PgC yr⁻¹), with respiration parameterisation as the dominant source of model divergence.

## Summary

- **Problem:** GPP estimates are broadly consistent across process-based (TRENDY) and data-driven EC-upscaling methods (~130 PgC yr⁻¹), but net carbon uptake estimates span 6–26 PgC yr⁻¹, pointing to ecosystem respiration as the unresolved bottleneck. No prior study had delivered a globally consistent, observationally grounded estimate of full carbon dynamics including stocks.
- **Method:** UFLUX v2e — a three-step framework: (1) semi-empirical baseline models for GPP, RECO, NEE, LE constrained by physiological laws; (2) machine-learning residual refinement against 206 FLUXNET2015 EC towers using SmartForest4D (XGBoost); (3) emulation of TRENDY carbon allocation ratios (Ra, Rh, NPP, vegetation/litter/soil stocks) via 10+ ML models trained on all 21 TRENDY v12 models. Monthly, 0.5°, 2003–2022. Bayesian inference used to attribute climate drivers.
- **Key result:** UFLUX GPP ≈ 130 PgC yr⁻¹ growing at 0.48 PgC yr⁻¹; NEP ≈ 16 PgC yr⁻¹ (TRENDY mean ≈ 4 PgC yr⁻¹; pure upscaling ≈ 25 PgC yr⁻¹). RECO in UFLUX ≈ 100 PgC yr⁻¹ vs. TRENDY ≈ 120 PgC yr⁻¹ — the ~20 PgC yr⁻¹ gap explains most of the NEP divergence. In tropical regions, GPP trend +0.1 PgC yr⁻¹ is offset by RECO trend +0.05 PgC yr⁻¹. UFLUX biomass stocks (cVeg ~400 PgC) are ~14% lower than TRENDY (~460 PgC). Validation r² ≈ 0.8 for all four fluxes; r² > 0.9 for most carbon stocks when emulating TRENDY mean.
- **Why it matters:** Pinpoints respiration parameterisation — not photosynthesis — as the primary source of cross-model divergence in terrestrial carbon budgets. Provides the first observation-constrained global dataset of full carbon dynamics (fluxes + stocks) at monthly 0.5° for 20 years. Tropical tipping-point risk is quantified: rising respiration nearly cancels photosynthetic gains, challenging the assumption of a persistent tropical land sink.
- **Caveats:** FLUXNET tower sampling is sparse in tropical Africa and high latitudes, inflating extrapolation uncertainty there. Carbon stock validation relies on ESA CCI AGB (r² = 0.70, RMSE 19 MgC ha⁻¹), which itself carries methodological uncertainty. UFLUX emulates TRENDY allocation ratios rather than simulating allocation mechanistically, so errors in the TRENDY ensemble propagate. Soil carbon (cSoil) has the largest absolute uncertainty (nRMSE ~16%).
