# First global carbon dynamics from an observational and process-informed hybrid perspective: Oversimplified respiration representation likely drives divergence in terrestrial carbon sequestration across models

**Authors:** Songyan Zhu, Wenquan Dong, Vasileios Myrgiotis et al.
**Venue:** Agricultural and Forest Meteorology · **Date:** 2026-04-27 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.agrformet.2026.111197
**Score:** 9/10 top pick
**Source:** full text

## TL;DR

A process-informed ML hybrid (UFLUX) constrains global carbon fluxes with EC observations, pinning net uptake at 16 PgC yr⁻¹ — between process-model (6) and pure-upscaling (26) estimates — with respiration parameterisation driving the gap.

## Summary

- **Problem:** Process-based models and data-driven EC upscaling diverge by >50 PgC yr⁻¹ in gross fluxes and yield net carbon uptake estimates ranging from 6 to 26 PgC yr⁻¹, with no consensus on which is more reliable.
- **Method:** UFLUX v2e hybrid: semi-empirical baseline models (LUE for GPP, Lloyd-Taylor for RECO) refined against 206 FLUXNET2015 EC towers using SmartForest4D (deep-layer XGBoost), then emulates carbon allocation ratios from 21 TRENDY models; Bayesian inference for climate sensitivity; 20-year global coverage (2003–2022) at 0.5° monthly.
- **Key result:** Global GPP ~130 PgC yr⁻¹ in 2022 (rising 0.48 PgC yr⁻¹ annually, consistent across methods); UFLUX net uptake 16 PgC yr⁻¹ vs. 6 (TRENDY) vs. 26 (conventional upscaling). RECO ~20 PgC yr⁻¹ lower in UFLUX than TRENDY; divergence traced to respiration parameterisation. Validation r² ~0.8 for all four fluxes vs. EC. In tropical regions, rising photosynthesis (+0.1 PgC yr⁻¹) is nearly offset by rising respiration (+0.05 PgC yr⁻¹), flagging tipping-point risk.
- **Why it matters:** First observationally anchored, full carbon cycle global estimate enforcing mass conservation; quantifies that respiration — not GPP — is the main source of model divergence in land carbon sink estimates; directly relevant for IPCC budget reconciliation.
- **Caveats:** EC training sites under-represent tropical Africa and high-latitude permafrost; belowground biomass stocks poorly constrained; TRENDY allocation ratios as learning targets introduce some circularity.
