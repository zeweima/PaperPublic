# Escalating Compound Drought‐Heatwaves and Demographic Shifts Threaten Simultaneous Global Breadbasket Failures

**Authors:** Amitesh Sabut, Ashok Mishra
**Venue:** Geophysical Research Letters · **Date:** 2026-04-24 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1029/2025gl118650
**Score:** 9/10<sup> top pick</sup>
**Source:** full text

## TL;DR

Compound drought-heatwave frequency, duration, and severity in global breadbaskets are projected to triple, quadruple, and quadruple by 2095 under SSP5-8.5, with simultaneous stress across three or more regions reaching 0.47 probability — amplified by population aging in producing countries.

## Summary

- **Problem:** CDHW events in major breadbaskets (Indo-Gangetic Plain, Central US, Central Europe, NE China, S. American Pampas, Canadian Prairies) are rising, but the risk of *simultaneous* multi-breadbasket failure and compounding demographic pressure is unquantified.
- **Method:** Historical CDHW events (1982–2019) from GPCC precipitation and CPC temperature data compared against MME of 8 CMIP6 GCMs (SSP1-2.6, SSP2-4.5, SSP5-8.5) for 2020–2095, at 0.25° × 0.25°. CDHW defined as coincident sc-PDSI drought (<10th percentile) and heatwave (T_max > 95th percentile for ≥3 consecutive days). Breadbaskets delineated via GGCP10 wheat production + DBSCAN clustering. Demographic shifts analyzed using UN WPP 2024 Medium Variant projections and COMTRADE trade linkages.
- **Key result:** Under SSP5-8.5 by 2095: frequency ~3× historical, duration ~4× (~35 days), severity ~4× (~100 vs. ~20). Indo-Gangetic Plain sees the largest changes (+2 events/yr, +40 days duration). Probability of simultaneous high-stress CDHWs in ≥5 breadbaskets rises to ~0.47 by 2090. Central Europe + NE China + Indo-Gangetic Plain co-occurrence probability reaches 0.77 (SSP5-8.5, far future). Dependency ratios in supply regions rise 1.4× (Canadian Prairies) to 2.56× (Central Europe) by century's end, with elderly share (60+) growing from 21% to 37% in Central European producing countries.
- **Why it matters:** Simultaneous breadbasket stress under high emissions could trigger synchronized global yield losses, price spikes, and food insecurity — especially for food-import-dependent nations whose supply chains run through aging producer regions. Demographic aging in supplier countries further constrains adaptive capacity.
- **Caveats:** Analysis covers only climatic drivers; irrigation intensity, land management, and geopolitical trade shifts not modeled. Dependency ratios use total working-age population rather than actual agricultural workforce, likely underestimating vulnerability in mechanized systems. Climate models may underestimate land–atmosphere feedback strength and synchronicity of future CDHWs.
