# Freeze-thaw perturbation decreases priming of soil organic carbon mineralization induced by straw return in croplands

**Authors:** Meng Na, Mengqin Xiang, Yue Wang et al.
**Venue:** Soil Biology & Biochemistry · **Date:** 2026-04-21 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.soilbio.2026.110180
**Score:** 8/10<sup> top pick</sup>
**Source:** full text

## TL;DR

A single freeze-thaw cycle cuts straw-induced SOC priming by 35% in cropland Mollisols by releasing labile C and N that reduce microbial nutrient mining and disrupt copiotrophic community succession.

## Summary

- **Problem:** Straw return stimulates SOC mineralization via microbial priming, but whether seasonal freeze-thaw cycles modify this priming effect in high-latitude agroecosystems is unknown.
- **Method:** 40-day laboratory microcosm incubation with C₃ rice straw (±mineral N) added to C₄ maize Mollisols (Northeast China) that were either kept continuously moist at 20 °C (unperturbed) or subjected to a single −20 °C freeze (3 days) followed by thaw at 20 °C. ¹³C-CO₂ isotope partitioning separated straw-derived from SOM-derived C fluxes; 16S rRNA and ITS amplicon sequencing on days 3 and 40 tracked bacterial and fungal community responses (24 microcosms total).
- **Key result:** Cumulative SOC priming was 35% lower in freeze-thaw soils with straw alone versus unperturbed soils — equivalent in magnitude to adding mineral N in unperturbed soils. Freeze-thaw increased soil NH₄⁺ and NO₃⁻ (~30–60% on early sampling days), POC (+7%), and DOC (+5%), consistent with aggregate disruption releasing labile C and N and thereby alleviating microbial N demand from SOM. Microbial biomass C fell ~20–30% post-freeze-thaw. In unperturbed soils, priming correlated positively with copiotrophs (Bacilli, Gammaproteobacteria, Mortierellomycota early; oligotrophic Polyangia, Bacteroidia, Parachaetomium late), reflecting a copiotrophic-to-oligotrophic succession driving strong N-mining. Freeze-thaw selectively favoured freeze-tolerant taxa (Kitasatospora, Cladosporium) and resilient copiotrophs (Massilia, Sphingomonas), disrupting this succession and dampening late-stage oligotrophic priming.
- **Why it matters:** Seasonal freeze-thaw disturbances reshape microbial life-history strategies in straw-amended soils, potentially moderating native SOC losses from cold-region croplands after residue return; this mechanism links climate-driven disturbance frequency to agroecosystem C budgets.
- **Caveats:** Single lab freeze-thaw event; C₃/C₄ isotope partitioning may under-attribute MAOC priming given >10 years of C₄ cultivation without full isotopic equilibrium. Microbial functional roles inferred from correlations, not enzymatic or metagenomic assays. Field validation under repeated natural freeze-thaw cycles with native crop residues is needed.
