# [Catchment precipitation partitioning in the Budyko framework is controlled by root zone storage capacity](https://doi.org/10.1088/1748-9326/ae612a)

**Authors:** Muhammad Ibrahim, Fransje van Oorschot, Ruud van der Ent et al.
**Venue:** Environmental Research Letters · **Date:** 2026-04-17 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1088/1748-9326/ae612a
**Score:** 10/10<sup> top pick</sup>
**Source:** abstract

## TL;DR

Root zone storage capacity (S_r,max) is the dominant control on Budyko curve deviations across >5000 global catchments, stronger than all other tested controls.

## Summary

- **Problem:** The Budyko framework predicts long-term precipitation partitioning from the aridity index, but catchments systematically deviate from the global-mean curve; the drivers of those deviations (the ω parameter in Fu's equation) remain debated.
- **Method:** S_r,max estimated via the memory method (runoff observations + water balance) for >5000 catchments globally; Spearman correlations between ω and S_r,max computed within Köppen-Geiger climate zones.
- **Key result:** S_r,max shows the strongest global correlation with ω (Spearman's ρ = 0.68), exceeding all other tested controls. Correlations are highest in cold (ρ = 0.87) and Mediterranean (ρ = 0.83) climates, and lowest in arid climates (ρ = 0.61).
- **Why it matters:** Establishes a theoretical basis for maximum S_r,max values constrained by Budyko water-energy limits; links vegetation rooting depth adaptation to catchment-scale ET/runoff partitioning globally; supports using S_r,max as a parameterization target in LSMs.
- **Caveats:** S_r,max is derived indirectly from runoff and water-balance observations (memory method), not measured directly. Weaker relationships in arid climates suggest additional unresolved controls. Causality (vegetation adapting S_r,max vs. S_r,max driving partitioning) is not fully disentangled.
