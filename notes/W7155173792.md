# Soil organic nitrogen rather than fertilizer drives dinitrogen losses in flooded rice systems

**Authors:** Lei Y, Zhijun Wei, Kaiye Ye et al.
**Venue:** PNAS · **Date:** 2026-04-22 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.2603983123
**Score:** 9/10<sup> top pick</sup>
**Source:** abstract

## TL;DR

Soil organic N, not applied fertilizer, is the dominant driver of dinitrogen losses in flooded rice systems, and hybrid cultivars cut yield-scaled gaseous N losses 43%.

## Summary

- **Problem:** N budgets for flooded rice paddies have underestimated dinitrogen (N₂) losses by misattributing them to fertilizer inputs rather than native soil organic N mineralization.
- **Method:** <unclear from abstract> — inferred to involve isotope-tracing or mass-balance approaches in flooded rice field experiments with conventional and hybrid rice cultivars.
- **Key result:** Fertilization-driven priming of soil organic N increases N₂ losses by ~35%; hybrid rice boosts yield 59% and reduces yield-scaled gaseous N losses 43% via enhanced fertilizer uptake and microbial N use efficiency.
- **Why it matters:** Revises N budgets for flooded rice systems globally; cultivar selection (hybrid vs. conventional) emerges as a management lever that simultaneously improves productivity and reduces N₂/greenhouse gas emissions.
- **Caveats:** Abstract is truncated — full experimental design, soil types, and geographic scope unclear; generalizability beyond study sites not confirmed.
