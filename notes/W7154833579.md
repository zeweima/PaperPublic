# Positive biotic associations of microorganisms promote metabolic efficiency to drive organic carbon stabilization in saline soils

**Authors:** Kun Yan, Beibei Wang, Ziliang Zhang et al.
**Venue:** Geoderma · **Date:** 2026 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.geoderma.2026.117819
**Score:** 8/10
**Source:** full text

## TL;DR

Microbial positive biotic associations boost CUE and necromass production, explaining 72–88% of global-scale SOC stability variance in saline soils, with TN as the dominant regulator.

## Summary

- **Problem:** How soil microbial biotic associations govern mineral-associated organic carbon (MAOC) formation under salinization is unresolved; salinity is widely assumed to dominate SOC stability but leaves substantial variance unexplained.
- **Method:** Field sampling across 9 plant communities along a salinity gradient in the Yellow River Delta (0–20 cm topsoil); measured SOC fractions (MAOC, POC), microbial necromass C (MNC), CUE, microbial growth rate (MGR), qCO₂, C/N-cycle enzymes, iron oxides, and co-occurrence network-derived biotic association strengths (iDIRECT + QCMI algorithms). Validated with a global meta-analysis of 1,877 paired observations from 40 sites.
- **Key result:** Positive biotic associations explained 44% of MAOC/SOC variance; bacterial positive associations were the single strongest predictor of MAOC/SOC (random forest R² = 39.4%, p < 0.001). Microbial positive associations increased CUE (bacterial R² = 0.85; fungal R² = 0.72) and MNC (bacterial R² = 0.74; fungal R² = 0.77), which in turn promoted MAOC formation (bacterial R² = 0.88; fungal R² = 0.86). Soil salt suppressed positive associations and MAOC/SOC (R²-M = 0.47), while TN promoted them; TN explained 90%, 78%, and 74% of MAOC, MNC, and MNC/SOC variance respectively. Meta-analysis confirmed positive associations explain 72–88% of global SOC stability variance.
- **Why it matters:** Reframes saline soil C management: nitrogen availability, not just salt stress, is the key lever for activating microbial cooperative networks that drive the mineral carbon pump. Optimizing TN inputs in salinized ecosystems may strengthen MNC accumulation and MAOC stabilization.
- **Caveats:** Co-occurrence network associations are putative (correlation-based), not confirmed direct interactions. Meta-analysis skews toward Asia/Europe with few African or South American sites. Causality between positive associations and CUE improvement cannot be fully separated from confounding nutrient gradients.
