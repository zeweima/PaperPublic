# [Discovery of nitrogen-responsive microbial indicators as metrics of freshwater ecosystem health](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.watres.2026.125959)

**Authors:** Jonathan Warren, Caitlin de Vries, Laura Hunt et al.
**Venue:** Water Research · **Date:** 2026-04-17 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.watres.2026.125959
**Score:** 9/10<sup> top pick</sup>
**Source:** full text

## TL;DR

XGBoost on 16S rRNA biofilm metabarcoding identifies 156 bacterial genera as threshold indicators of oxidised nitrogen in English rivers, with sensitive taxa responding at 0.30 mg N L⁻¹ and tolerant taxa at 3.78 mg N L⁻¹.

## Summary

- **Problem:** Microorganisms are largely absent from regulatory freshwater monitoring (EU Water Framework Directive); no validated microbial metrics exist for nitrogen despite nitrogen being a co-limiting nutrient and key driver of biological community change in rivers.
- **Method:** 16S rRNA V4 metabarcoding of 1574 epilithic/epiphytic biofilm samples from 694 sites across England's river network (2021–2023), sampled up to 7× per site. XGBoost and Random Forest regression modelled nitrate-N and total oxidised nitrogen (TON) from genus-level relative abundances (699 genera after filtering). SHAP values ranked genus importance; TITAN2 threshold-indicator analysis identified community change points along the nitrogen gradient. Reference genomes were queried for nitrogen-cycling gene ontology terms to link indicator taxa to functional processes.
- **Key result:** XGB models explained R² = 0.654 (nitrate-N) and R² = 0.650 (TON) on held-out test data. Of 699 genera, 193 were important predictors; 156 of these were also threshold indicators. Sensitive community change point: 0.30 mg NO₃-N L⁻¹ (0.30 mg TON L⁻¹). Tolerant change point: 3.78 mg NO₃-N L⁻¹ (3.97 mg TON L⁻¹). Denitrification-associated genera (e.g., *Denitratisfoma*, *Hydrogenophaga*) were positively associated with nitrate-N; genera with competitive advantage at low N (e.g., *Anaero­myxobacter*) were negatively associated. Denitrification and nitrogen cycling gene pathway abundances were weakly but significantly correlated with predicted nitrate-N (r = 0.19, p < 0.0001).
- **Why it matters:** Establishes ecologically grounded nitrogen thresholds derived from microbial community responses — comparable in magnitude to thresholds reported for macroinvertebrates (~1–3 mg L⁻¹). Framework is extensible to other pressures and trophic levels, and could directly inform WFD ecological targets and national nitrogen criteria for rivers.
- **Caveats:** 16S-based functional inference (via reference genome GO terms) is coarser than metagenomics; only ~49% of genera had genus-level reference genomes. Biofilm integrates seasonal variability but temporal replication is uneven across sites. Ecological change points not yet translated into regulatory thresholds; typology-specific validation needed across different river classes.
