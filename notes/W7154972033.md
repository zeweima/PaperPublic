# Effects of Pesticide Mixtures and Environmental Factors on Benthic Diatom Communities: A Microcosm Approach

**Authors:** <unclear from abstract> et al.

**Score:** 8/10

## TL;DR

Pesticide mixtures (up to 4 combined) showed no community-level effect on benthic diatoms, but species-specific sensitivity patterns identify candidate bioindicators for Swiss stream pesticide assessment.

## Summary

- **Problem:** Ecotoxicological assessments rarely combine multiple pesticides with realistic environmental stressors simultaneously, leaving the community-level vs. species-level response question unresolved for diatom biomonitoring.
- **Method:** 25-day microcosm experiment crossing pesticide mixtures (up to 4 pesticides) with light intensity, temperature, and nutrient level treatments; diatom communities characterized by Next-Generation Sequencing; biofilm descriptors measured (beta-diversity, effective photosynthetic activity, chlorophyll-a, ash-free dry weight, bacterial abundance).
- **Key result:** No significant pesticide effects at the community level; species-specific sensitivity or tolerance patterns detected, yielding candidate bioindicator taxa toward a diatom-based pesticide stress index for Swiss streams.
- **Why it matters:** Demonstrates that community-aggregate metrics mask pesticide signals detectable only at the species level; supports development of targeted bioassessment indices for regulatory use.
- **Caveats:** Microcosm results may not fully represent field stream conditions; Swiss stream context may limit generalizability; specific pesticide identities and concentrations not reported in abstract.
