# Assessing the soil capacity to produce food and biomass worldwide

**Authors:** Nícolas Augusto Rosin, Alex B. McBratney, Raul Roberto Poppiel et al.
**Venue:** Geoderma · **Date:** 2026 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.geoderma.2026.117821
**Score:** 8/10
**Source:** full text

## TL;DR

First global 90-m map of soil capacity to produce food and biomass, derived from ecoregion-stratified GAM utility functions linking clay, pH, SOC, and PAW to area-weighted crop yields across five major crops.

## Key result

- Bivariate GAM R² ranged 0.01–0.36 per individual soil attribute; multivariate models using all attributes from both surface (0–20 cm) and subsoil (80–100 cm) layers reached R² 0.18–0.46, with RMSE 0.09–0.25.
- Spatial prediction R² of 0.64–0.69 (individual attributes) to 0.68 (all surface attributes) and 0.64 (all attributes, both layers); mean uncertainty 0.11–0.13.
- Highest predicted utility (U*pred) in northeastern North America, northern Europe, and parts of southeastern South America and central Africa (ecoregions 4 and 8); lowest in northern/southern Africa, Middle East, central Asia, western North America, and central Australia (ecoregion 13).
- Rainfed cropland sample median U* = 0.66, well above global all-land median, confirming existing agriculture concentrates on higher-capacity soils.
- Some utility functions showed unexpected behavior (e.g., high U*pred for sandy soils in several ecoregions), attributed to uncontrolled non-soil drivers in yield data (management, climate, socioeconomics).

## Summary

- **Problem:** No global, soil-attribute-based assessment of soil capacity to produce food and biomass existed; prior soil security frameworks lacked spatially explicit, data-driven utility functions linking intrinsic soil properties to crop yield at global scale.
- **Method:** Soil Security Assessment Framework (SSAF) applied to five crops (sugarcane, maize, rice, wheat, soybean) using ~10-km yield and harvested area data (Yu et al. 2020, rainfed systems only). Crop yields converted to a unitless utility via empirical CDF, aggregated by area-weighted mean to a single U*. Soil attributes (clay, pH, SOC, PAW) harmonized to 0–20 and 80–100 cm layers from WoSIS (~251,903 points). Bivariate and multivariate GAMs fitted per ecoregion (14 ecoregions; 10 used after data filtering). Final U*pred maps generated at 90-m resolution via Random Forest DSM in GEE using SCORPAN environmental covariates; bootstrapped 100×.
- **Key result:** See above.
- **Why it matters:** First approximation of the soil availability dimension of food security at global scale; 90-m resolution enables downscaling to farm level and extrapolation to non-agricultural land with matching soil and climate conditions, supporting land-use planning and soil conservation prioritization.
- **Caveats:** Low bivariate GAM R² (many <0.20) reflects strong non-soil signal in yield data; subsoil data globally sparse (limited bootstrapping points for both-layer models); utility function behavior departs from agronomic expectation in several ecoregions; framework captures rainfed systems only; input soil maps carry their own spatial uncertainty that cascades through predictions.
