# Enhancing winter wheat yield estimation by synergizing UAV remote sensing and a hybrid PROSAIL-AquaCrop data assimilation framework

**Authors:** Yang Xu, Xiaobo Gu, Jingya Liu et al.

## TL;DR
A hybrid PROSAIL-AquaCrop data assimilation framework fused with UAV imagery improves winter wheat yield estimation accuracy over standalone crop models or remote sensing alone.

## Summary
- **Problem:** Accurate within-field wheat yield estimation is limited by the gap between canopy radiative-transfer models (PROSAIL) that retrieve biophysical traits from UAV imagery and process-based crop models (AquaCrop) that simulate yield; neither alone is sufficient at fine spatial scales.
- **Method:** <unclear from abstract> — framework couples UAV-derived multispectral/hyperspectral canopy reflectance through PROSAIL inversion to retrieve LAI and/or canopy cover, which are then assimilated into AquaCrop to update yield predictions; tested on winter wheat plots/fields.
- **Key result:** <unclear from abstract> — quantitative accuracy metrics (R², RMSE) for yield estimation not retrievable from available metadata.
- **Why it matters:** Combining physically-based radiative transfer inversion with a data-driven crop model via data assimilation provides a scalable, interpretable pathway for high-resolution yield mapping, relevant for precision agriculture and food security monitoring.
- **Caveats:** Scope limited to winter wheat; UAV operational range constrains scalability to field/farm scale; generalisability to other crops, climates, or satellite sensors untested; no full text available to verify quantitative claims.
