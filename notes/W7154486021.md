# [Cloud feedback overestimated in coupled CESM2 simulations relative to AMIP simulations and satellite observations: importance of the pattern effect and implications for climate sensitivity](https://doi.org/10.1088/1748-9326/ae5fab)

**Authors:** Shiv Priyam Raghuraman, Brian Medeiros
**Venue:** Environmental Research Letters · **Date:** 2026-04-15 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1088/1748-9326/ae5fab
**Score:** 9/10<sup> top pick</sup>
**Source:** abstract

## TL;DR

CESM2's coupled ocean-atmosphere run inflates cloud feedback beyond satellite observations due to an East Pacific SST pattern effect, validating the model's high equilibrium climate sensitivity as physically plausible.

## Summary
- **Problem:** CESM2 has anomalously high equilibrium climate sensitivity driven by large cloud feedback; whether this bias is realistic or a model artifact is unresolved.
- **Method:** Compared cloud radiative effects in CESM2 AMIP runs (prescribed observed SSTs/sea ice, 2002–2022) versus fully coupled runs over the same period, benchmarked against satellite observations; applied process-based decomposition to isolate feedback components.
- **Key result:** AMIP-mode CESM2 reproduces satellite cloud observations within uncertainty; coupled-mode feedback is substantially larger, driven by excessive East Pacific surface warming, enhanced Walker circulation weakening, and reduced lower-tropospheric stability that causes thick low clouds to decrease more than observed, reflecting less sunlight.
- **Why it matters:** The SST pattern effect — not intrinsic atmospheric model error — explains the coupled bias; and because present-day coupled SST patterns resemble future equilibrium warming patterns, CESM2's high climate sensitivity may in fact be realized if that SST pattern emerges at equilibrium.
- **Caveats:** Analysis covers only 2002–2022 (one climate state); conclusions depend on the fidelity of satellite cloud retrievals and on whether the East Pacific warming pattern is model-specific or broadly shared across CMIP6 models.
