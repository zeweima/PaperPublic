# [Unveiling the Disproportionate Impact of Nitrogen Deposition on Aquatic Nutrient Loading and Greenhouse Gas Emissions](https://doi.org/10.1021/acs.est.6c02887)

**Authors:** Weixiang Li, Shudian Peng, Shuai Li et al.
**Venue:** Environmental Science & Technology · **Date:** 2026-04-15 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1021/acs.est.6c02887
**Score:** 9/10<sup> top pick</sup>
**Source:** abstract

## TL;DR

Nitrogen deposition drives disproportionate aquatic nutrient loading and GHG emissions in agricultural watersheds, and NH₃/NOₓ reductions have not kept pace with other pollutant controls.

## Summary

- **Problem:** Atmospheric nitrogen deposition links air pollution to freshwater quality and aquatic greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions, but the magnitude and dynamics of this pathway in agricultural watersheds remain poorly quantified.
- **Method:** Watershed-scale analysis linking nitrogen deposition inputs to water-quality nutrient loading and aquatic N₂O (and likely CO₂/CH₄) emissions; specific watershed location and time period unclear from abstract fragment.
- **Key result:** Reductions in nitrogen deposition have lagged far behind decreases in other atmospheric pollutants in the study watershed; nitrogen deposition exerts a disproportionate effect on both aquatic nutrient loading and GHG emissions relative to its share of total atmospheric pollutant reductions.
- **Why it matters:** Calls for a co-control strategy targeting atmospheric NH₃, acidic gases, and NOₓ together, integrated with climate change mitigation, rather than addressing air quality and water quality in isolation.
- **Caveats:** Abstract is truncated — specific quantitative results (% changes, magnitudes) are unclear from available text; single-watershed focus limits generalizability; causal attribution between deposition and aquatic GHG fluxes needs verification across systems.
