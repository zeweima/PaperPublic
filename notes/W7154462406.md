# [Rising rainfall variability amplifies hydroclimate extremes in eastern China](https://doi.org/10.1088/1748-9326/ae5fb4)

**Authors:** Lin Pei, Shiguang Miao, Deliang Chen et al.
**Venue:** Environmental Research Letters · **Date:** 2026-04-15 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1088/1748-9326/ae5fb4
**Score:** 9/10<sup> top pick</sup>
**Source:** abstract

## TL;DR

Warming shifts eastern China's rainfall spectrum from light to intense events, projecting a 29.4% rise in precipitation volatility by 2100 under high emissions.

## Summary

- **Problem:** How increased rainfall variability under global warming translates into compound drought-flood hazards at the regional scale in eastern China.
- **Method:** Long-term observed daily precipitation records combined with multiple decadal convection-permitting climate simulations; rainfall spectrum analysis and a precipitation volatility index (PVI) combining precipitation intensity with dry spell duration; projected to end-of-21st-century under high-emission scenario.
- **Key result:** PVI increases 29.4% by end of 21st century; spectral shift from light to intense precipitation driven by more frequent high CAPE and CIN conditions, amplified by a stronger East Asian summer monsoon circulation.
- **Why it matters:** Simultaneous amplification of flash-flood and drought risk demands integrated water management strategies rather than single-hazard approaches; convection-permitting simulations validate the spectral shift mechanism.
- **Caveats:** Findings focused on eastern China; generalizability to other monsoon regions unclear; reliance on a single combined index (PVI) may obscure asymmetric drought vs. flood contributions.
