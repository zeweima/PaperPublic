# Dominant Role of Soil Moisture Regulating Vegetation Water Content During Flash Drought Evolution

**Authors:** Amitesh Gupta, Lanka Karthikeyan, Ashok Mishra et al.
**Venue:** Geophysical Research Letters · **Date:** 2026 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1029/2025gl119366
**Score:** 8/10
**Source:** full text

## TL;DR

Rootzone soil moisture dominates over VPD in driving vegetation water content loss and recovery across all plant functional types during flash droughts globally.

## Summary

- **Problem:** Relative importance of concurrent vs. antecedent soil moisture (SM) and vapor pressure deficit (VPD) in controlling vegetation hydraulic state across flash drought phases and plant functional types (PFTs) is unresolved.
- **Method:** Satellite vegetation optical depth (VOD; AMSR-E/AMSR2, 2003–2021) as a proxy for vegetation water content, paired with GLEAM rootzone SM (10–100 cm) and ERA5 VPD at 0.25° resolution globally. Flash droughts identified by SM-percentile criteria across four phases (onset, development, recovery, near-normal). Phase-resolved impact metric (elasticity of VOD to SM or VPD) and normalized transfer entropy (NTE) for causal antecedent analysis across three PFTs (forests, croplands, grasslands-shrublands) and aridity/isohydricity gradients.
- **Key result:** SM impact on VOD exceeds VPD impact across all PFTs and phases. VOD sensitivity to SM is highest in water-limited phases; grasslands-shrublands show greatest sensitivity and steepest VOD decline at onset. Over isohydric forests, SM has 3.0x greater antecedent influence than VPD during dry-down phases; in anisohydric forests, VPD has 1.9x greater antecedent influence during wet-up phases. Cropland VOD sensitivity to SM increases through drought progression (onset < development < recovery < near-normal), opposite to natural ecosystems. Antecedent SM effects exceed antecedent VPD effects across PFTs, with the gap widening during recovery-to-near-normal transition, especially in drier and shallow-rooted systems. 7 out of 23 SREX regions account for 55% of global flash drought occurrences.
- **Why it matters:** Establishes SM — not atmospheric demand — as the primary lever controlling vegetation water status during flash droughts; cropland delayed recovery signals yield-loss risk. Region- and PFT-specific frameworks are needed for flash drought impact assessment and mitigation strategies.
- **Caveats:** Near-normal threshold fixed at 40th SM percentile; alternative thresholds may shift estimated SM/VPD influence magnitudes. Irrigated and rainfed croplands not separated. VOD conflates biomass and water content at inter-seasonal scales; complementary ecosystem proxies recommended. Causal analysis cannot fully isolate confounding land-atmosphere feedbacks under water limitation.
