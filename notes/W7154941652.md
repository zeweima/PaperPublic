# Quantifying Under-Ice Phytoplankton Blooms in the Changing Arctic and Southern Oceans

**Authors:** Courtney M. Payne, Alice K. DuVivier, Kristen M. Krumhardt et al.
**Venue:** Geophysical Research Letters · **Date:** 2026 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1029/2026gl121750
**Score:** 8/10
**Source:** full text

## TL;DR

Under-ice phytoplankton generate ~100 Tg C yr⁻¹ in each polar hemisphere but shift poleward and decline in importance by 2100 under accelerating sea ice loss.

## Summary

- **Problem:** Satellite-derived NPP estimates cannot detect productivity under sea ice, leaving under-ice (UI) phytoplankton blooms unquantified at hemispheric scale and their future trajectory unknown.
- **Method:** Single CESM2 fully coupled Earth system model simulation (SSP3-7.0, 1920–2100) with sub-grid sea ice thickness partitioned into five categories; hourly light limitation computed per ice category to apportion total NPP to under-ice fraction. Arctic and Southern Ocean hemispheres both evaluated.
- **Key result:** UI NPP averages 104 Tg C yr⁻¹ (Northern Hemisphere) and 92 Tg C yr⁻¹ (Southern Hemisphere) between the 1920s and 2090s — ~100 Tg C yr⁻¹ each, invisible to remote sensing. Diatoms account for 85% of UI NPP in both hemispheres. In the NH, UI NPP rises slightly through the 2030s then declines 9.4 Tg C yr⁻¹ decade⁻¹ to 2090s; Arctic Circle contribution drops from 20% to 13% of annual NPP. In the SH, UI NPP is stable through 2050s then falls 5.4 Tg C yr⁻¹ decade⁻¹. UI blooms shift poleward: Arctic shelf dominance (48% of UI NPP in 1920s) gives way to deep Arctic Ocean (44% by 2030s); Antarctic shelf share rises from 17% to 25% by 2090s. Arctic blooms form under thicker, consolidated ice via melt-pond light transmission; Southern Ocean blooms form under thinner marginal-ice-zone ice where snow inhibits melt-pond formation.
- **Why it matters:** Satellite-derived polar NPP estimates are underestimated by >100 Tg C yr⁻¹ per hemisphere. Poleward shifts reduce food availability to benthic Arctic ecosystems and may alter the Southern Ocean biological pump; UI bloom declines by century's end represent a significant reorganization of polar carbon cycling.
- **Caveats:** Single model simulation; no phytoplankton adaptation or explicit sea ice algae; SH sea ice variability high and model may not capture post-2016 Antarctic regime shift; light limitation overestimate of 8.9–13.6% introduced by hourly curve approximation.
