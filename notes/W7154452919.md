# [Quantitative apportionment of multiple water sources and their implications for coastal nutrient dynamics: A mult-tracer framework of river-dominated system](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.watres.2026.125949)

## TL;DR

Combining stable isotopes and rare earth elements in an OMP framework resolves 7 water sources in the Changjiang estuary and links river discharge quantitatively to coastal nutrient stoichiometry.

## Summary

- **Problem:** Coastal nutrient dynamics in river-dominated estuaries depend on multiple overlapping freshwater sources (large rivers, small rivers, precipitation, groundwater, shelf water), but conventional temperature-salinity mixing diagrams cannot discriminate them — leaving nutrient budgets and stoichiometry poorly constrained.
- **Method:** Optimum Multi-Parameter (OMP) analysis combining stable water isotopes (δ¹⁸O, δD) and 11 dissolved rare earth elements (PAAS-normalized) as conservative tracers across the Changjiang Estuary–Zhejiang coastal area (East China Sea); seasonal cruises 2024–2025; sediment porewater extracted from 0–3 cm cores; seven endmembers identified (CDW, HZBW, OJW, JJW, MSW, MSSW, PW).
- **Key result:** Changjiang Diluted Water dominates north of 30.5°N with contributions >60%. Smaller Jiaojiang and Oujiang rivers, despite limited volume, control nearshore nutrient stoichiometry: river fraction (f_River) correlates with DIN/DIP (r = 0.88, P < 10⁻⁵) and inversely with DISi/DIN (r = −0.63, P < 10⁻⁵). Precipitation contributes 2–8% seasonally (up to 23% during Typhoon events). Muddy sediment porewater shows strong anthropogenic Gd anomalies (Gd/Gd* up to 1.37), marking coastal muds as a major contaminant sink. A logarithmic relationship links river discharge Q to plume half-contribution radius R₅₀: R₅₀ = 55.1 × ln(Q + 41.6) − 174 (r² = 0.998).
- **Why it matters:** Provides a transferable, source-explicit diagnostic framework that (1) moves coastal management from qualitative source ID to quantitative attribution, (2) links discharge directly to the spatial extent of eutrophication and hypoxia risk, and (3) validates hydrodynamic-biogeochemical models with source fractions. Shows that small rivers disproportionately shape nearshore fishery habitat nutrient quality.
- **Caveats:** OMP results depend on endmember stability; non-conservative REE behavior (Ce oxidation, Gd anthropogenic anomalies) required exclusion/correction. Sediment grain-size heterogeneity means the porewater Gd sink characterization applies specifically to muddy southern Zhejiang stations. The R₅₀–Q relationship is empirical and may not transfer to other estuarine geometries.
