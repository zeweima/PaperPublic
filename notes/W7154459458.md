# [Framework for global emulation of extreme precipitation along global warming trajectories](https://doi.org/10.1088/1748-9326/ae5fad)

**Authors:** Lorenzo Pierini, Lukas Gudmundsson, Yann Quilcaille et al.
**Venue:** Environmental Research Letters · **Date:** 2026-04-15 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1088/1748-9326/ae5fad
**Score:** 9/10<sup> top pick</sup>
**Source:** abstract

## TL;DR

MESMER-X-based emulator reproduces CMIP6 extreme precipitation statistics globally at a fraction of ESM cost, enabling large-ensemble risk assessment across warming trajectories.

## Summary

- **Problem:** Reliable assessment of rare, high-impact precipitation extremes under warming requires large ensembles, but full Earth system model runs are computationally prohibitive; existing emulators lack adequate treatment of arid-region near-zero maxima and heavy-tailed tropical distributions.
- **Method:** Extended MESMER-X statistical emulation framework trained on CMIP6 multi-model output; produces spatially resolved annual maximum daily precipitation time series conditioned on global mean temperature trajectories; includes dedicated handling of arid-region behavior and heavy-tailed tropical extremes.
- **Key result:** Emulator faithfully replicates CMIP6 key statistical properties, spatial patterns, natural variability, and global warming responses; enables rapid large-ensemble probabilistic exploration of extreme precipitation across emission scenarios at near-zero marginal cost per ensemble member.
- **Why it matters:** Bridges the gap between computationally cheap pattern-scaling and full ESM ensembles for extreme precipitation; directly supports policy-relevant linkages between emission pathways and changes in flood-generating precipitation extremes at regional to global scales.
- **Caveats:** Trained on CMIP6 model spread—inherits their structural biases; performance in regions with sparse CMIP6 agreement or unusual statistical distributions (arid zones, deep tropics) treated with special assumptions that may not hold under strong warming; sub-daily extremes not addressed.
