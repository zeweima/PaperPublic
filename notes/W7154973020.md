# Algal Interaction-Mediated Biogenic Volatiles Enable Accurate Algal Bloom Prediction

## TL;DR

Interaction-associated algal volatile organic compounds — especially terpenoids like DMNT — predict bloom biomass better than metabolism-linked volatiles, validated in a natural lake.

## Summary
- **Problem:** Algal bloom prediction lacks reliable early-warning signals; whether algal biogenic volatiles (AVOCs) driven by interspecific interactions improve prediction over baseline metabolic VOCs is untested.
- **Method:** Combined AVOC profiling with transcriptomic analyses to partition volatiles into fundamental-metabolism-associated vs. interaction-associated categories; tested predictive importance of each class; validated DMNT–biomass correlation in a natural lake.
- **Key result:** Interaction-associated AVOCs (notably terpenoids) showed a 123% increase in predictive importance over fundamental-metabolism-associated AVOCs. DMNT content correlated significantly with algal biomass in field validation.
- **Why it matters:** Terpenoid stress-signaling volatiles could serve as non-invasive, airborne indicators for early bloom forecasting; shifts prediction paradigm from water-column chemistry toward atmospheric volatile signatures.
- **Caveats:** Field validation limited to one natural lake; causal mechanism between DMNT production and biomass accumulation not fully established; seasonal and species-composition effects on AVOC profiles unclear from abstract.
