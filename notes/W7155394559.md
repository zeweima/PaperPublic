# Long-term organic manure substitution enhances soil carbon stability via organically complexed iron oxides: Evidence from a 12-year field experiment

**Authors:** Yiming Xian, Xingyu Li, Rong Huang et al.
**Venue:** Geoderma · **Date:** 2026-04-23 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.geoderma.2026.117825
**Score:** 9/10 <sup>top pick</sup>
**Source:** full text

## TL;DR

In a 12-year paddy field trial, 100% organic N substitution maximized MAOC via Fe_PP-mediated co-precipitation without increasing greenhouse gas emissions.

## Summary

- **Problem:** Sequential extraction studies of iron oxides in soil have focused on bulk soil, leaving fraction-specific Fe-OC dynamics within the mineral-associated organic matter (MAOM) fraction unclear under varying organic manure substitution ratios.
- **Method:** 12-year rice–wheat rotation field experiment (Sichuan, China) with four N-management treatments: 0% (CF), 25% (OS25), 50% (OS50), and 100% (OS100) organic N substitution using pig manure compost. Soil density fractionation isolated MAOM; sequential selective dissolution (pyrophosphate, hydroxylamine-HCl, dithionite-HCl) quantified organically complexed (Fe_PP), amorphous (Fe_HH), and crystalline (Fe_DH) iron oxides and their associated OC in both bulk soil and MAOM. Linear mixed-effects models and automated linear modeling identified dominant predictors of MAOC/SOC ratio.
- **Key result:** OS100 increased SOC 11.23% and MAOC 23.73% vs. CF. Organic substitution drove crystalline-to-poorly-crystalline Fe oxide transformation, with Fe_PP becoming dominant in MAOM (proportion rising from 38.80% to 54.22%). OC_PP showed a non-linear dose response: depressed at OS25–OS50, then sharply elevated at OS100 (13.53–35.22% above other treatments). OC/Fe molar ratios for Fe_PP (14.46–15.21 in bulk; 20.00–22.74 in MAOM) far exceeded the co-precipitation threshold of 6, supporting co-precipitation as the dominant C-stabilization mechanism at OS100. Total greenhouse gas emissions (CO₂ + CH₄) showed no significant treatment differences despite OS50 peaking mid-range; OS100 achieved maximum C stabilization without concomitant emission increase. Fe_PP-related variables were the dominant predictors of MAOC/SOC in MAOM (cumulative importance 0.74 vs. 0.79 for crystalline Fe variables in bulk soil).
- **Why it matters:** Identifies the MAOM fraction—not bulk soil—as the critical locus for Fe-mediated carbon stabilization, and establishes that high-rate (≥50%) organic substitution activates Fe_PP co-precipitation as a route to durable SOC accrual without climate penalty. Provides mechanistic justification for targeting Fe_PP accumulation in management recommendations for Fe-rich paddy soils.
- **Caveats:** Single site (Fe-rich Anthrosol, periodically flooded); results may not transfer to well-aerated oxic upland soils where crystalline Fe dominates. POC estimated as SOC − MAOC rather than by direct fractionation. Threshold for Fe_PP formation along organic input gradients and long-term stability of co-precipitated complexes under hydrological extremes remain unquantified.
