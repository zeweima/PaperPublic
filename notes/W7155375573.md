# Physiological process-based feature engineering enables robust estimation of crop gross primary production under data-limited conditions

**Authors:** Mizuki Horikoshi, Gaku Yokoyama, Shigehiro Kubota et al.
**Venue:** Agricultural and Forest Meteorology · **Date:** 2026-04-23 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.agrformet.2026.111191
**Score:** 9/10 <sup>top pick</sup>
**Source:** full text

## TL;DR

A hybrid ANN using physiologically derived leaf-level photosynthesis rates as inputs generalizes to unseen crop sites and drought conditions where conventional ANNs catastrophically fail.

## Summary

- **Problem:** ML models for cropland GPP estimation overfit to training site conditions; conventional ANNs degrade badly when extrapolated to new climates or extreme drought, limiting large-scale applicability.
- **Method:** Hybrid ANN (ANNh) — environmental variables (PPFD, T_a, VPD, W_s, C_a, ψ_soil) are first converted to normalized leaf-level photosynthetic rates (A) via C₄/C₃ photosynthesis biochemistry, energy balance, and stomatal conductance process models; A and EVI then feed the ANN. Compared against a direct-input ANN (ANNs) and two LUE baselines (EC-LUE, MODIS) across 8 maize and 6 wheat FLUXNET2015/ICOS sites using 5-fold CV and single-site training validation (SSTV).
- **Key result:** Under 5-fold CV, ANNs led (maize R² = 0.91, RMSE = 4.49 µmol m⁻² s⁻¹) but ANNh matched or exceeded LUE baselines. Under SSTV (data-limited), ANNs collapsed (maize R² = −0.26, RMSE = 13.8 µmol m⁻² s⁻¹); ANNh stayed robust (R² = 0.78, RMSE = 6.87 µmol m⁻² s⁻¹), comparable to EC-LUE (RMSE = 6.42) and MODIS (RMSE = 8.32). During the 2005 US-ARM drought, ANNs produced physically implausible GPP (R² = −301, RMSE = 59.6 µmol m⁻² s⁻¹); ANNh remained accurate (R² = 0.36, RMSE = 1.78).
- **Why it matters:** Process-based feature engineering acts as a domain-knowledge regularizer, embedding temperature and water stress constraints directly into the input space rather than relying on L2/dropout alone — a path to GPP models that generalize under novel extreme climates without large multi-site training datasets.
- **Caveats:** ANNh uses only two input variables (A, EVI), missing complex canopy dynamics (light interception, canopy architecture, cultivar effects); EVI quality is sensitive to satellite retrieval uncertainties; process model parameters (V_cmax, J_max) are from literature and differ by fertilization/cultivar; tested on maize (C₄) and wheat (C₃) only.
