## TL;DR

A scale-adaptive mapping approach quantifies expanding wildland–human interface globally, revealing rising fire and ecological threat exposure to human communities.

## Summary
- **Problem:** The wildland–human interface (WHI) — where human settlements meet or intermix with wildland vegetation — is a key zone of wildfire risk, yet prior global delineations lack scale sensitivity and may misrepresent the true extent and trend of human exposure.
- **Method:** Scale-adaptive spatial approach applied globally to characterize the WHI; integrates settlement and vegetation/land-cover data to delineate interface zones at ecologically relevant scales.
- **Key result:** <unclear from abstract>
- **Why it matters:** Reveals that WHI extent and associated threats (wildfire, ecological disruption) are larger and growing faster than previously recognized, with implications for land management, fire policy, and climate adaptation planning.
