# Decoding Microbial Reductive Dechlorination of 209 Polychlorinated Biphenyl Congeners through Experiment-Aided Quantum Chemistry and Machine Learning

**Authors:** Shanquan Wang, Haozheng He, Shangwei Zhang et al.
**Venue:** Environmental Science & Technology · **Date:** 2026-04-24 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1021/acs.est.5c16226
**Score:** 8/10<sup> top pick</sup>
**Source:** abstract

## TL;DR
Combining quantum chemistry, steric corrections, and XGBoost achieves 98.3% accuracy predicting which chlorine positions Dehalococcoides removes across all 209 PCB congeners.

## Summary
- **Problem:** Reductive dechlorination pathways and reactivity of all 209 PCB congeners by anaerobic bacteria (Dehalococcoides) were incompletely characterized, limiting bioremediation predictions for toxic, dioxin-like congeners.
- **Method:** High-throughput enzymatic assays coupled with Hirshfeld charge analysis (quantum chemical descriptor for electron density at chlorine sites) and empirically derived steric parameters; XGBoost models trained on electronic, steric, and physicochemical descriptors to quantify dechlorination reactivity across diverse Dehalococcoides isolates and enrichment cultures.
- **Key result:** Pathway prediction accuracy of 98.3% across all 209 congeners; steric-corrected Hirshfeld charge and PCB aqueous solubility identified as primary controls on dechlorination potential; 11 of 12 dioxin-like congeners predicted susceptible to microbial reductive dechlorination.
- **Why it matters:** Provides a full-coverage, quantitative framework for predicting PCB bioremediation outcomes — particularly for the most toxic dioxin-like congeners — without needing exhaustive wet-lab screening of each congener.
- **Caveats:** Model trained on Dehalococcoides; generalizability to other dechlorinating taxa unclear. Solubility as a key descriptor may limit predictions in non-aqueous or sorption-dominated sediment environments. Experimental validation covers only a subset of the 209 congeners.
