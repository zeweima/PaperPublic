# Methane production potential is higher in newly restored depressional wetlands compared to natural and older restored counterparts

**Authors:** Nora Hamovit, Stephanie Yarwood
**Venue:** Geoderma · **Date:** 2026 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.geoderma.2026.117814
**Score:** 9/10
**Source:** full text

## TL;DR

18-year-old restored Delmarva Bay wetlands have ~30x higher CH4 production potential than natural or >30-year restored sites, driven by herbaceous vegetation and Fe availability.

## Summary

- **Problem:** Wetland restoration raises CH4 emissions relative to natural reference sites, but how long this elevation persists and what edaphic controls govern it remain unresolved for depressional freshwater wetlands.
- **Method:** Soil incubations (top 15 cm mineral soil) from 3 natural, 3 eighteen-year (18y), and 3 thirty-plus-year (+30y) restored Delmarva Bay wetlands on the Mid-Atlantic Coastal Plain, USA; measured potential CH4 production, aerobic oxidation, and anaerobic oxidation (AOM) rates; qPCR of *mcrA* (methanogens) and *pmoA* (methanotrophs); environmental covariates including total Fe, SOM, herbaceous vegetation cover, water level, and organic layer depth.
- **Key result:** Potential CH4 production was 30x higher in 18y restorations (20.78 ± 47.36 µmol g⁻¹ dwt soil h⁻¹) than in natural (0.66 ± 2.03) or +30y restorations (0.30 ± 0.54); AOM rates were significantly lower in +30y sites than in 18y and natural sites; *mcrA* copies were elevated in both 18y and +30y vs. natural, but *pmoA* copies were highest in +30y; best predictors of CH4 production were site identity, total Fe, and herbaceous vegetation cover (adj. R² up to 0.58 in multivariate models).
- **Why it matters:** Newly restored wetlands may act as CH4 hot spots for ~20 years post-restoration; the trajectory toward natural reference conditions is non-linear and site-specific, complicating GHG accounting for wetland restoration as a climate solution. Crystalline Fe availability and primary production appear more important controls than restoration age per se.
- **Caveats:** Only 9 sites total; sampling time differed between 18y (beginning of growing season) and +30y (end of season), potentially inflating the contrast; potential rates from lab incubations cannot be directly equated to in situ fluxes; Delmarva Bay system may not generalize to other wetland types or regions.
