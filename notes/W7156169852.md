# A Geographically Weighted Gaussian Process Regression (GW-GPR) emulator of anthropogenic PM2.5 from the GEOS-Chem High Performance (GCHP) 13.0.0 global chemical transport model

**Authors:** Anthony Y. H. Wong, Sebastian D. Eastham, Erwan Monier et al.
**Venue:** Geoscientific Model Development · **Date:** 2026-04-27 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.5194/gmd-19-3335-2026
**Score:** 9/10 <sup>top pick</sup>
**Source:** full text

## TL;DR

A GW-GPR emulator of GCHP reproduces global grid-cell PM2.5 responses (R²=0.94–0.99) to emission and climate scenarios in <10 s vs. ~3000 CPU hours.

## Summary

- **Problem:** Full chemical transport models (e.g., GCHP 13.0.0) require ~2400–3000 CPU hours per scenario run, blocking their use in integrated assessment, ensemble modelling, and policy analysis; existing reduced-form alternatives rely on linear source-receptor matrices that cannot capture non-linear secondary aerosol chemistry or sub-regional emission patterns.
- **Method:** Geographically Weighted Gaussian Process Regression trained on 120 Latin-Hypercube-sampled perturbation experiments from GCHP-CAM. Inputs are geographically blurred changes in 7 air pollutant emission species (NOx, SO2, NH3, NMVOC, BC, OC, CO) plus atmospheric CH4 and CO2 concentration (as proxy for global warming / TRF). A separate GPR is fit at each populated grid cell (~2° × 2.5°). Validated via 10-fold cross-validation and tested against four IGSM-GAINS-TAPS climate/air-quality scenarios at 2050; further benchmarked against AerChemMIP ensemble (SSP3-7.0 and SSP3-7.0-lowNTCF).
- **Key result:** Cross-validation: R²=0.97, MAE=0.356 µg m⁻³, mean bias=−0.012 µg m⁻³ globally. Scenario evaluation: R²=0.94–0.99, MAE=0.20–0.42 µg m⁻³ across four 2050 scenarios; 58.7–84.8% of grid cells agree with GCHP within 1 emulator SD. Premature-mortality impacts agree with GCHP within 1.2%. GPR outperforms equivalent multilinear regression (MLR) in all scenarios, with MAE reductions of 0.05–0.10 µg m⁻³ in regions with non-linear aerosol chemistry. Inference time: ~10 CPU seconds per scenario vs. ~3000 CPU hours for GCHP.
- **Why it matters:** First global-scale PM2.5 emulator operating at grid-cell resolution with explicit non-linearity and calibrated uncertainty estimates; enables rapid ensemble/scenario analysis and integration into IAMs. AerChemMIP validation shows emulator falls within inter-model spread, confirming fitness for multi-model benchmarking.
- **Caveats:** Climate effects parameterized via CO2/TRF proxy—breaks down under overshoot scenarios or when CO2 trend decouples from other GHGs. Emulator does not include SOA, dust, sea salt (anthropogenic PM2.5 definition only). Largest errors over Northern China and Northern India (MAE up to 5 µg m⁻³) where base-year emissions are highest. Sahel region shows consistent high bias. Performance under spatially heterogeneous emission changes (outside training range) needs further testing.
