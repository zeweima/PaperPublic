# Moisture Budgets and Circulation Analogs: Diagnosing Dynamic and Thermodynamic Precipitation Change

**Authors:** Robert Doane-Solomon, Isla R. Simpson, Tim Woollings
**Venue:** arXiv (physics.ao-ph) · **Date:** 2026-04-24 · **DOI:** n/a
**Score:** 8/10<sup> top pick</sup>
**Source:** full text

## TL;DR

Combining moisture budget analysis with constructed circulation analogs reduces dynamic contamination and yields weaker thermodynamic precipitation decline projections for Central Chile than either method alone.

## Summary

- **Problem:** Moisture budget decomposition and dynamical adjustment via constructed circulation analogs (CCAs) are both used to separate dynamic (circulation-driven) and thermodynamic (moisture-driven) precipitation trends, but the two methods have never been directly compared on the same region and variable, and it is unclear whether they produce consistent physical interpretations.
- **Method:** Both a Reynolds-decomposition moisture budget (VIMC = vertically integrated moisture flux convergence) and a CCA dynamical adjustment technique applied to the CESM2 100-member Large Ensemble (SSP370 to 2100) plus ERA5 and JRA-3Q reanalyses over Central Chile (30–42°S, 70–74°W), wet season MJJA, 1980–2022 and 1980–2100. CCAs use SLP anomaly matching (N_a = 50, N_s = 30, N_r = 100) against a fixed 1980–2022 climatology to prevent thermodynamic contamination of the dynamic term.
- **Key result:** Dynamic trend estimates from the two methods agree in sign in the ensemble mean but differ substantially in magnitude; individual ensemble members frequently disagree. Applying CCAs directly to moisture budget terms reduces dynamic contamination of the thermodynamic signal, and the resulting combined framework yields thermodynamic precipitation projections that are weaker than those from either moisture budget or CCA alone. VIMC correlates with P at r > 0.99 over Central Chile, and ~80% of monthly VIMC variance is captured by monthly-mean winds (submonthly transients explain the remainder).
- **Why it matters:** Establishes that method choice can materially alter the inferred dynamic vs. thermodynamic split of regional precipitation decline — relevant for attributing the ongoing Central Chile megadrought and for constraining future projections in Mediterranean-type climates more broadly. The combined framework offers a pathway to cleaner thermodynamic estimates by leveraging the complementary strengths of each approach.
- **Caveats:** Case study limited to Central Chile (and supplementary South Australia); CCA performance depends on somewhat arbitrary choices of N_a, N_s, N_r, and SLP domain; moisture budgets may not close over orographic terrain; the method cannot fully separate submonthly dynamic variability from the thermodynamic residual.
