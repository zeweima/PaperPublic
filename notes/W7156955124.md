# Nonlinear Responses Between Watershed Hydrological Functional Connectivity and Rainfall‐Runoff‐Sediment

**Authors:** Yaojun Liu, Yu Long, Ming Lei et al.
**Venue:** Water Resources Research · **Date:** 2026-04-28 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1029/2025wr042706
**Score:** 9/10<sup> top pick</sup>
**Source:** full text

## TL;DR

A threshold IHC mean of 0.53 separates low-connectivity subsurface-dominated events from high-connectivity surface–subsurface coupled events that drive disproportionate sediment export.

## Summary

- **Problem:** How hydrological functional connectivity varies across rainfall types and how it nonlinearly controls runoff generation and sediment yield in humid hilly watersheds — a mechanism gap not resolved by structural connectivity indices alone.
- **Method:** 221 rainfall events (2017–2024) in the Longyan watershed (2.19 km², southern China red-soil hills) classified via K-means into Type I (long-duration light, n=150), Type II (short-duration storm, n=23), Type III (long-duration heavy, n=48). End-Member Mixing Analysis (EMMA) using δ²H and δ¹⁸O isotopes partitioned pre-event vs. event water contributions. Functional connectivity quantified with the Index of Hydrosedimentological Connectivity (IHC), an IC-index extension incorporating antecedent rainfall, current rainfall, and surface runoff via the SCS model. Spatial mapping in ArcMap 10.2.
- **Key result:** Type III events generated 3.16× the runoff volume and highest mean SY (7.52 t) and SR (4.50 t km⁻²) of all types. Pre-event water dominated Types I and II (73.09% and 71.84% of runoff), while event water dominated Type III (54.33%). Mean IHC: Type III = 1.19, Type II = 0.34, Type I = −0.05. A nonlinear threshold at IHC mean = 0.53 separates low-connectivity (subsurface-flow dominated, limited sediment transport) from high-connectivity regimes (surface–subsurface coupling, strong sediment transport). Type III events accounted for only 21.72% of events but 46.69% of total runoff and 72.76% of total SY.
- **Why it matters:** Identifies a measurable IHC threshold that can spatially locate high-erosion-risk areas and trigger timely soil-water conservation interventions; shifts soil loss management framing from rainfall amount to connectivity state.
- **Caveats:** Single small watershed (2.19 km²) in subtropical red-soil hilly terrain; transferability to other climates, lithologies, or larger watersheds untested. EMMA assumes two end-members; additional flow paths (interflow) may be conflated. SCS-CN parameterization adds uncertainty to IHC calculation.
