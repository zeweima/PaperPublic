# Differential Drivers of Thermogenic and Bulk Dissolved Organic Matter in the Indian Ocean

**Authors:** Tassiana S. G. Serafim, Heike Simon, Jutta Niggemann et al.
**Venue:** Global Biogeochemical Cycles · **Date:** 2026-04-28 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1029/2025gb008975
**Score:** 8/10<sup> top pick</sup>
**Source:** full text

## TL;DR

DBC and PAHs decouple in the Indian Ocean water column: PAHs degrade with oxygen consumption while DBC shows no AOU correlation, confirming combustion-derived condensed aromatics are ultra-refractory.

## Summary

- **Problem:** Thermogenic DOM (dissolved black carbon, DBC; polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons, PAHs) distribution and transformation in the Indian Ocean — a global overturning circulation hub — were unconstrained; no DBC dataset existed for this basin.
- **Method:** 176 water samples collected along a surface transect (5°N–20°S) and 11 full depth profiles (to 5,425 m) during R/V SONNE expedition SO305/2 (July–August 2024, summer monsoon). DBC quantified via benzenepolycarboxylic acids (BPCA/UPLC); 16 EPA-priority PAHs by GC-MS after SPE; bulk DOM by FT-ICR-MS (4,500 molecular formulae per sample). AOU used as proxy for remineralization age.
- **Key result:** Surface DBC ranged 0.32–0.99 µmol L⁻¹ (mean 0.72 ± 0.11 µmol L⁻¹), homogeneously distributed with no regional pattern; PAHs (2.94–8.10 ng L⁻¹) were significantly higher in the southern Indian Ocean. Below the mixed layer, PAH concentrations decreased significantly with depth (R² = 0.06, p = 0.016) and correlated weakly with AOU (rS = −0.46, p = 0.004), while DBC showed no AOU correlation (rS = −0.08, p > 0.1). Bulk DOM degradation index (I_DEG) and Island of Stability (IOS) both increased with AOU (IOS R² = 0.27, p < 0.001). DBC:DOC ratio (0.07–2.52%) increased with depth (R² = 0.30, p < 0.001), indicating preferential accumulation of DBC relative to bulk DOC. Indian Ocean surface DBC (0.72 µmol L⁻¹) exceeded North Central Pacific (0.06 µmol L⁻¹) and northwestern Pacific (0.23 µmol L⁻¹) surface values, attributed to Ganges-Brahmaputra-Meghna riverine input redistributed by the monsoon.
- **Why it matters:** Demonstrates that DBC behaves as a conservative, ultra-refractory tracer decoupled from oxygen-driven remineralization, while PAHs are semi-labile and lost below the mixed layer. The Indian Ocean acts as a net integrator of atmospheric and riverine thermogenic inputs, exporting refractory DBC to deep water via overturning circulation — relevant for the global marine refractory DOC reservoir budget.
- **Caveats:** Single expedition during summer monsoon only; inter-seasonal variability in monsoon-driven riverine inputs not captured. PAH depth trend explains only 6% of variance (R² = 0.06), suggesting additional controls (particle sorption, photodegradation) are unresolved. DBC quantification via BPCA is an operational metric for condensed aromatics, not a sum of discrete molecules.
