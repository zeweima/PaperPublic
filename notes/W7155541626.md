# The Value of Forecasters-in-the-Loop in Real-Time Flood Forecasting in the Age of Machine Learning

**Authors:** Vinh Ngoc Tran, Donghui Xu, Phong Le et al.
**Venue:** Geophysical Research Letters · **Date:** 2026-04-24 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1029/2025gl118317
**Score:** 9/10<sup> top pick</sup>
**Source:** full text

## TL;DR

Operational human-in-the-loop flood forecasting (CNRFC/CHPS) beats LSTM-based ML at all lead times up to 96 hr, even when ML uses observed rather than forecast precipitation.

## Summary

- **Problem:** ML flood forecasting studies benchmark against reanalysis baselines or simplified models, not against real operational systems with trained human forecasters — creating an inflated picture of ML readiness.
- **Method:** Multi-frequency LSTM models (ML_DI with data integration; ML_Naive without) trained on CAMELSH hourly data were compared against CNRFC operational forecasts (Community Hydrologic Prediction System + human adjustment) at 50 California/Nevada stations, 2012–2022, 1–96 hr lead times. ML models used observed NLDAS-2 forcing; CNRFC used biased 6-hr QPF. Metrics: NSE, KGE, Peak Error, POD/CSI/FAR for flood alerts.
- **Key result:** CHPS outperformed ML_DI at 80%, 86%, 90%, and 92% of locations at 12, 24, 48, and 72 hr lead times. At 48-hr lead, CHPS produced fewer false alarms than ML_Naive at 15 locations vs. only 8 locations generating more. CHPS achieved NSE > 0.5 at 38 locations at 96 hr; ML_DI matched that at only 4. CHPS operational forecasts using biased precipitation inputs significantly outperformed NWM3.0 retrospective simulations using observed forcings on NSE and KGE metrics across all lead times.
- **Why it matters:** Current ML benchmarking against retrospective simulations systematically overstates operational utility; the field needs to benchmark against actual operational forecasts from river forecast centers. Human forecasters compensate for poor precipitation inputs through basin-specific knowledge and manual state corrections — a capability current ML architectures cannot replicate in real time. Forecasters-in-the-loop with ML assistance (ensemble/hybrid approaches) is more promising than full automation.
- **Caveats:** Human adjustments in CHPS are not separately quantified, so the forecaster contribution cannot be isolated from model calibration effects. Study covers one region (CA/NV) with one ML architecture (LSTM); results may differ elsewhere or with other ML variants. ML_DI training used single-step-ahead loss, causing rapid skill degradation at extended lead times — sequence-to-sequence training could narrow the gap.
