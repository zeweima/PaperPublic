# The Value of Forecasters-in-the-Loop in Real-Time Flood Forecasting in the Age of Machine Learning

**Authors:** Vinh Ngoc Tran, Donghui Xu, Phong Le et al.
**Venue:** Geophysical Research Letters · **Date:** 2026-04-24 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1029/2025gl118317
**Score:** 9/10<sup> top pick</sup>
**Source:** full text

## TL;DR
Human-guided operational forecasting (CNRFC/CHPS) consistently outperforms LSTM models across all lead times up to 96 hr, even when ML uses observed rather than forecast forcings.

## Summary
- **Problem:** Most ML flood-forecasting benchmarks compare against simplified retrospective simulations, not real operational systems with human experts adjusting outputs in real time — inflating perceived ML skill.
- **Method:** Multi-frequency LSTM models (ML_DI with data integration; ML_Naive without) trained on CAMELSH data were benchmarked against CNRFC operational forecasts at 50 California/Nevada stations, 2012–2022, at 1–96 hr lead times. ML used observed NLDAS-2 forcings; CNRFC used biased real-time precipitation forecasts. Metrics: NSE, Peak Error, POD, CSI, FAR at Action and Flood thresholds.
- **Key result:** CHPS outperforms ML_DI at 80%, 86%, 90%, and 92% of locations at 12, 24, 48, and 72 hr lead times respectively (NSE basis). At 48-hr lead time, CHPS exceeds ML_Naive on POD at 16 of 50 Action-threshold locations and generates fewer false alarms at 15 locations vs. 8 where ML is better. CHPS operational forecasts using biased precipitation inputs also significantly outperform NWM3.0 retrospective simulations driven by observed forcings across all lead times. Only at 1-hr lead time does ML_DI outperform CHPS at 68% of locations.
- **Why it matters:** Directly challenges the narrative that ML has surpassed operational hydrological forecasting; establishes that forecaster expertise compensates for poor precipitation inputs in ways current automated ML cannot replicate. Recommends benchmarking future ML against actual operational forecasts from regional river forecast centers rather than retrospective simulations.
- **Caveats:** Forecaster contributions within CHPS are assumed but not directly quantified (no baseline PBM-only run without human adjustment). Study uses a single LSTM architecture; other ML variants or sequence-to-sequence training may narrow the gap. CHPS benefits from basin-specific calibration and reservoir data not available to the ML models, though headwater/regulated basin sub-analyses still favor CHPS.
