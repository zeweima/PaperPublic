# A novel memory-based deep learning framework for reliable joint estimation of daily vegetation carbon fluxes

**Authors:** Ximing Chen, Jiejun Huang, Han Zhou et al.
**Venue:** Agricultural and Forest Meteorology · **Date:** 2026-04-24 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.agrformet.2026.111198
**Score:** 9/10 <sup>top pick</sup>
**Source:** full text

## TL;DR

DemLSTM, a dual-encoder multi-task LSTM, jointly estimates GPP, RECO, and NEE with improved accuracy and physical consistency over single-task and tree-based baselines.

## Summary

- **Problem:** Most carbon flux models treat GPP, RECO, and NEE independently, ignore vegetation memory effects (lagged responses to antecedent climate), and lack physical consistency — FLUXCOM's median carbon closure bias is 0.174 g C m⁻² day⁻¹.
- **Method:** DemLSTM uses two independent LSTM encoders (one for meteorology/soil, one for remote-sensing vegetation indices) feeding a fusion layer with three parallel output heads for simultaneous GPP, RECO, and NEE prediction. Granger causality tests determine PFT-specific look-back periods (15–55 days). Trained on FLUXNET2015 + AmeriFlux (213 sites, 447 k site-days across 9 PFTs); transferability tested on ICOS Warm Winter (73 European sites). XAI gradient attribution and linear probe analysis (against PML-V2 transpiration and canopy conductance) assess reliability.
- **Key result:** Site-based cross-validation R² = 0.743 / 0.625 / 0.471 for GPP / RECO / NEE; generalization R² = 0.698 / 0.594 / 0.497 — exceeding vanilla LSTM, RF, and XGBoost on all three fluxes. Median carbon closure bias reduced to 0.025 g C m⁻² day⁻¹ vs. 0.406–0.455 for RF and XGBoost. Linear probe correlation for transpiration: DemLSTM median 0.89 vs. vanilla LSTM 0.85; for canopy conductance: 0.85 vs. 0.84–0.85. Meteorology encoder correlations (0.87 / 0.81) exceed vegetation encoder (0.68 / 0.73), confirming lagged climate dominates carbon dynamics.
- **Why it matters:** Joint multi-task learning enforces GPP–RECO–NEE self-consistency and embeds carbon–water coupling without explicit process constraints, substantially reducing closure bias across PFTs and improving transferability to out-of-sample European sites.
- **Caveats:** Look-back periods are PFT-level medians, not site-specific; ERA5-based soil moisture and SOC introduce reanalysis uncertainty; linear probe reliability metric depends on PML-V2 transpiration estimates which carry their own errors; performance in MF (mixed forest) and SH (shrublands) remains weakest.
