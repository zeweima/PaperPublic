# [Underestimated agricultural losses due to flooding](https://doi.org/10.1126/sciadv.aed2754)

**Authors:** Shulei Zhang, Liming Zhou, Hongbin Liang et al.
**Venue:** Science Advances · **Date:** 2026-04-15 · **DOI:** 10.1126/sciadv.aed2754
**Score:** <unclear from abstract>/10
**Source:** full text

## TL;DR

A flood-stress adjustment to GGCMI3 crop models reveals flood-induced agricultural losses are comparable to or exceed drought losses in many regions under high-emission scenarios.

## Summary

- **Problem:** Global crop models (GGCMI3) capture drought-induced yield losses but systematically fail to represent flood-induced losses — biases in simulated yield response to extreme wet conditions reached 26.7%, 28.3%, and 21.3% for maize, soybean, and wheat respectively — leaving future flood-crop risks poorly quantified.
- **Method:** A flooding stress algorithm couples CaMa-Flood global river-floodplain hydrodynamics (0.5° daily) with GGCMI3 crop simulations. Flood impact on yield is encoded as a dimensionless fractional loss coefficient (f_adj) driven by annual maximum 5-consecutive-day floodplain water depth during the growing season, modulated by crop-specific waterlogging vulnerability and soil hydraulic properties. Calibrated on US county-level maize, soybean, and wheat yield and insurance indemnity data (1980–2015); validated against USDA-RMA payments and global EM-DAT flood losses. Future projections use GGCMI Phase 3b + CMIP6 under SSP126 and SSP585.
- **Key result:** Adjusted simulations reproduce observed flood-induced yield reductions and align with USDA-RMA indemnities (Spearman R = 0.62, 0.47, 0.34 for maize, soybean, wheat at national scale; ~71% of countries captured ≥2 of top-5 global loss years). Under SSP585, projected annual flood losses reach ~$287 M (maize), $234 M (soybean), and $212 M (wheat) in the US by 2015–2100, with loss^EW anticipated to surpass loss^ED in ~40, 27, and 55% of US states. Globally, wheat losses in some Central Asian countries could reach ~1.0‰ of GDP; South American per-capita soybean losses are 20–30× the global average in Argentina, Paraguay, and Bolivia.
- **Why it matters:** Establishes that standard crop model ensembles grossly understate flood risk; reframes flood mitigation as co-equal to drought adaptation for food security planning. Framework is extendable to other crops and directly usable in ISIMIP-type impact assessments.
- **Caveats:** Flood-stress algorithm is empirical (not mechanistic), calibrated on US data only and not independently validated elsewhere. Static farm management and harvested area assumed through 2100. Economic estimates use simplified price assumptions; trade and policy feedbacks excluded. CaMa-Flood validated at 97% accuracy vs. satellite inundation but uncertainties remain in floodplain parameterization at 0.5° resolution.
