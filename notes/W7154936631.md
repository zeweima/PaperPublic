# MarineTox Predictor: An Online Library Platform for Enhancing Low-Resourced Saltwater Ecotoxicity Prediction via Knowledge Sharing from Freshwater Ecotoxicity

## TL;DR

Transfer learning from freshwater ecotoxicity improves saltwater toxicity prediction up to 140%, flags 902 chemicals of marine concern across ~68,000 substances.

## Summary

- **Problem:** Marine ecotoxicity datasets are sparse, limiting reliable prediction of chemical hazards to saltwater organisms and hindering marine ecological risk assessment.
- **Method:** Knowledge transfer from freshwater ecotoxicity data to a saltwater prediction model; interaction network analysis across multiple species and chemical substructures to identify toxicity-driving features; systematic derivation of environmental hazard thresholds for ~68,000 chemicals; results compiled into an online library platform (MarineTox Predictor).
- **Key result:** Up to 140% improvement over a model trained solely on saltwater data; six key substructures linked to toxicity toward specific marine organisms identified; 902 chemicals flagged as toxicity concern for the marine ecosystem; platform covers 1.2 million ecotoxicity records and hazard thresholds.
- **Why it matters:** Provides a scalable, publicly accessible resource for marine ecological risk assessment of diverse chemicals, directly applicable to regulatory screening where marine data are absent.
- **Caveats:** Transfer learning performance depends on mechanistic similarity between freshwater and marine species endpoints; hazard thresholds for ~68,000 chemicals are model-predicted, not empirically measured; platform coverage and accuracy for poorly characterized chemical classes unclear from abstract.
