## TL;DR

Mongolian pine relies on groundwater to maintain transpiration, but groundwater access cannot fully buffer water stress when precipitation declines.

## Summary

- **Problem:** Whether groundwater connectivity can compensate for precipitation-driven water stress in dryland afforestation species (Mongolian pine, *Pinus sylvestris* var. *mongolica*), which is widely planted in semi-arid northern China.
- **Method:** <unclear from abstract>
- **Key result:** Groundwater supports high transpiration rates in Mongolian pine, but its buffering capacity is insufficient to offset water stress when precipitation is experimentally or observationally reduced.
- **Why it matters:** Highlights a ceiling on groundwater-mediated drought resilience for plantation forests in semi-arid regions; informs sustainable afforestation planning under projected precipitation declines in dryland Asia.
