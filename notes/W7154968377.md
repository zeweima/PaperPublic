# Fungal biomass and guild dominance jointly govern soil organic carbon pools in alpine treeline ecosystems

**Authors:** Qiuyan Tan, Leho Tedersoo, Luyao Song et al.
**Venue:** Geoderma · **Date:** 2026 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.geoderma.2026.117817
**Score:** 8/10
**Source:** full text

## TL;DR

Tree species identity, not elevation, drives fungal guild structure that governs SOC accumulation and persistence in alpine treelines via ectomycorrhizal dominance.

## Summary

- **Problem:** How fungal biomass and guild composition regulate SOC pools in alpine treeline ecotones is poorly understood, particularly whether total biomass or guild-specific dominance matters more for SOC content vs. persistence.
- **Method:** 120 rhizosphere soil samples from two contrasting treeline ecotones at Baima Snow Mountain, SW China — *Abies georgei* (AG, 3768–4385 m) and *Larix potaninii* (LP, 4036–4534 m). Combined high-throughput ITS sequencing with ergosterol quantification to estimate total and guild-specific fungal biomass (EcMF, SAPF, PPF). OLS regression, structural equation modeling (SEM), 20 soil physicochemical variables, 10 root traits.
- **Key result:** Total fungal biomass showed no significant elevational trend in either ecotone; guild patterns differed by tree species. Fungal biomass was the strongest predictor of SOC content in LP (R²_adj = 0.342), explaining 18.2% of variation. SOC persistence increased significantly with relative EcMF dominance (PPF/EcMF ratio negatively affected persistence, SPC = −0.24 in AG; EcMF/SAPF ratio positively associated in LP, SPC = 0.24). SOC persistence did not vary with total EcMF biomass but increased significantly with the relative dominance of EcMF across both ecotones combined. EcMF biomass explained 74.8% of variance in AG vs. 30.2% in LP (R²_adj); soil C and N were primary drivers of fungal biomass in both ecotones.
- **Why it matters:** Long-term SOC stabilization in alpine systems depends more on EcMF guild dominance (MAOC pathway via necromass and mineral binding) than on absolute fungal biomass; tree species selection for conservation and restoration should prioritize EcMF-associated hosts to sustain carbon persistence under climate warming.
- **Caveats:** Guild-specific biomass estimated via ergosterol × relative sequence abundance assumes equal ergosterol content per unit biomass across guilds — values are approximate. Two treeline species occupy opposite slopes, confounding potential slope aspect effects. MAOC/POC ratio used as static proxy for SOC persistence cannot be directly equated to carbon residence time. PPF relative abundance was only 0.2%, limiting PPF conclusions.
