# Seasonal dynamics of closed shallow lakes nutrient status controlled by lacustrine groundwater discharge

**Authors:** Xiaoliang Sun, Yao Du, Hao Tian et al.
**Venue:** HESS (Hydrology and Earth System Sciences) · **Date:** 2026-04-24 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.5194/hess-30-2357-2026
**Score:** 9/10 <sup>top pick</sup>
**Source:** full text

## TL;DR

In a closed Yangtze oxbow lake, lacustrine groundwater discharge (LGD) peaks in winter (35–52 mm d⁻¹) and drives seasonal lake N, P, and N/P cycles that control chlorophyll-a more than temperature or light.

## Summary

- **Problem:** Intermonthly variability of lacustrine groundwater discharge (LGD) and its control on lake nutrient status are poorly constrained; most prior work used low-frequency (seasonal) snapshots insufficient to capture intra-seasonal dynamics.
- **Method:** Bi-monthly high-frequency monitoring over a full hydrological year (June 2022–July 2023) at Heiwawu Oxbow Lake, central Yangtze Basin (a closed, surface-runoff-isolated system). ²²²Rn mass balance model used to quantify LGD; 241 water samples analyzed for TN, TP, Chl-a, and water quality parameters. Eight groundwater monitoring wells plus lake water level gauges provided continuous hydraulic data.
- **Key result:** LGD rates ranged 35.36–51.71 mm d⁻¹ (peak December 2022), following a summer-to-winter increase then winter-to-spring decline. Monthly net precipitation (PME) controlled lake level, which set the hydraulic gradient to groundwater and hence LGD (R² = 0.86 with PME, nonlinear). LGD-TN loads ranged 10.76–24.00 mmol m⁻² d⁻¹; LGD-TP loads 0.29–0.92 mmol m⁻² d⁻¹, 1–2 orders of magnitude greater than atmospheric deposition or sediment diffusion. Lake TN correlated with LGD-TN (R² = 0.79); lake TP with LGD-TP (R² = 0.89). LGD-driven N/P ratio variation regulated lake water N/P (R² = 0.82), which in turn controlled Chl-a concentration more strongly than temperature or light.
- **Why it matters:** First intermonthly-scale evidence that groundwater-driven nutrient loading sets lake trophic status in closed shallow lakes; framework generalizable to similar lacustrine systems worldwide. High-frequency LGD observations reveal dynamic mechanisms (stage-dependent hydraulic gradient control) invisible to low-frequency sampling, with direct implications for eutrophication early warning and management.
- **Caveats:** Single-lake study in an unusually isolated (disconnected from river) system; 2022 drought accelerated disconnection by ~2 months, making 2022–2023 not a strictly typical hydrological year. Small sample size (n = 5 time points for mass-balance LGD) limits statistical robustness of fitted equations; TP dynamics also influenced by internal geochemical processes (adsorption, co-precipitation) not fully resolved.
