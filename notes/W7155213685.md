# ArchesWeatherGen: Skillful and compute-efficient probabilistic weather forecasting with machine learning

**Authors:** Guillaume Couairon, Renu Singh, Anastase Charantonis et al.
**Venue:** Science Advances · **Date:** 2026-04-22 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1126/sciadv.adx2372
**Score:** 9/10 <sup>top pick</sup>
**Source:** full text

## TL;DR

A two-stage flow-matching diffusion model built on top of deterministic ML forecasts surpasses IFS ENS and NeuralGCM on probabilistic WeatherBench metrics at 20× lower training cost.

## Summary

- **Problem:** Deterministic ML weather models produce smooth, physically inconsistent outputs that underrepresent forecast uncertainty; fully generative approaches trained from scratch require enormous compute.
- **Method:** Two-stage pipeline: (1) train a deterministic Swin-U-Net transformer (ArchesWeather) at 1.5° / 24-h lead on ERA5 with MSE loss; (2) train a flow-matching model (ArchesWeatherGen) on the residuals between deterministic predictions and true ERA5 states, projecting predictions onto the ERA5 distribution. A novel cross-level attention (CLA) module replaces Pangu-Weather's local 3D attention, enabling full vertical interaction at O(d²) parameter cost. Evaluated on WeatherBench 2 (2020 test year, 50-member ensembles).
- **Key result:** ArchesWeatherGen surpasses IFS ENS and NeuralGCM on ensemble mean RMSE, CRPS, energy score, and Brier score across all headline upper-air variables and lead times 1–10 days (except NeuralGCM geopotential at 3–10 days). CRPS skill ~5.3% above IFS ENS on average; roughly 2–4 percentage points better than DDPM baseline across all variables. CLA alone yields +7.4% RMSE skill score improvement over IFS HRES for the S-size model and +6% for M-size. Full training budget is 45 V100-days; inference is 3.5 s per 24-h forecast on an A100; deterministic ArchesWeather-M trains in ~9 V100-days vs. 256–16,128 for competing models.
- **Why it matters:** Demonstrates that probabilistic ML forecasting competitive with operational ensembles (IFS ENS) and physics-hybrid models (NeuralGCM) is achievable with academic-scale compute (~23 A100-days for best model), lowering the barrier for ensemble weather forecasting research and enabling score-based data assimilation use cases.
- **Caveats:** Performance lags 0.25° GenCast at short lead times (1–3 days); model operates at 1.5° so misses fine-scale features relevant to cyclone tracking and regional forecasting; underdispersion remains an issue without IC perturbations; non-Gaussian residuals complicate flow matching stability; geopotential advantage of NeuralGCM's dynamical core not fully overcome.
