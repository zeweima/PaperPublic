# [Quantifying the safe operating space for the Amazon rainforest under climate change and deforestation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27681)

**Authors:** Jonathan Krönke, Arie Staal, Jonathan F. Donges et al.
**Venue:** arXiv (physics.ao-ph) · **Date:** 2026-04-30 · **DOI:** n/a
**Score:** 9/10<sup> top pick</sup>
**Source:** full text

## TL;DR

At current 1.4 °C warming and 17 % deforestation, >30 % of the Amazon is already on the 20 % tipping isoline — the forest has likely left its safe operating space.

## Summary

- **Problem:** Amazon tipping-point analyses treat climate change and deforestation as independent stressors; their synergistic interaction via atmospheric moisture recycling means single-driver boundaries understate risk.
- **Method:** Reduced-complexity network model on a 416-cell 1°×1° grid; two stable states (forest / degraded) per cell; parameterized with NorESM2 output under SSP2-4.5, SSP3-7.0, SSP5-8.5; moisture-tracking algorithm couples cells via recycled rainfall; four spatial deforestation patterns tested (scenario-based, east-to-west, west-to-east, random); deforestation swept 0–50 %, warming 0–10 °C.
- **Key result:** Individual safe boundaries: 2 °C warming and 15 % deforestation (each alone causes 5 % tipping). Combined: present-day 1.4 °C + 17 % deforestation places the system on the 20 % tipping isoline, implying >30 % of forest area at high risk. Overall forest loss (deforestation + tipping) already reaches ~37 % under current conditions. Moisture recycling expands the tipping-dominated regime below 2 °C for deforestation 5–25 %, making the current state fall within the most dangerous region. The scenario-based (road-expansion) deforestation pattern is near-worst-case for low-to-moderate deforestation because it targets moisture-critical western regions first.
- **Why it matters:** Demonstrates that the Amazon may have already transgressed its planetary-boundary analog; quantifies a 2-D safe operating space that is more restrictive than either single-driver bound; supports halting deforestation as the more immediately actionable lever given saturation of tipping effects around 30 % deforestation.
- **Caveats:** Single ESM (NorESM2) for precipitation forcing; reduced-complexity model omits fire dynamics and carbon-cycle feedbacks explicitly; tipping thresholds calibrated from past variability, which may underestimate novel climate states; no explicit land-use recovery or reforestation scenarios tested.
