# Bundling conservation agriculture with drought-tolerant crops improves system productivity and stability in Zimbabwean smallholder systems

**Authors:** Christian Thierfelder, Blessing Mhlanga, Hambulo Ngoma
**Venue:** Field Crops Research · **Date:** 2026-04-20 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.fcr.2026.110487
**Score:** 8/10<sup> top pick</sup>
**Source:** full text

## TL;DR

CA with drought-tolerant maize hybrids and cowpea rotations raises maize yields 202–1104 kg ha⁻¹ yr⁻¹ and system energy 3–10% over conventional tillage across semi-arid Zimbabwe after a ~3-year transition.

## Summary

- **Problem:** Smallholder farmers in semi-arid Zimbabwe (450–600 mm annual rainfall) face declining productivity from drought, erratic rainfall, and sandy soil degradation; CA adoption remains ~10–15% nationally, partly because transition-period yield penalties deter uptake.
- **Method:** On-farm trials at 4 communities (Masvingo and Rushinga districts) 2018–2025, comparing CA vs. conventional tillage (CP) across maize varieties (PAN53, PGS63, MQ623, SC513, ZS242), sorghum, pearl millet, finger millet, cowpea, and groundnut. Linear mixed models, GGE biplot, temporal spline trajectories, quantile frontier analysis, and energy-yield calculations (GJ ha⁻¹) applied across up to 7 seasons.
- **Key result:** CA increased maize yields by 202–1104 kg ha⁻¹ yr⁻¹ after the 3-year transition; by year 7, CA trajectory slope was 625 kg ha⁻¹ yr⁻¹ vs. 263 kg ha⁻¹ yr⁻¹ for CP. System energy under CA was 26–27 GJ ha⁻¹ vs. 19–21 GJ ha⁻¹ under CP (3–10% gain). Cowpea outperformed groundnut as the rotation legume. Optimal maize stand ~55,000 plants ha⁻¹; cowpea ~90,000 plants ha⁻¹. PAN53 and PGS63 showed consistently superior and stable performance (PC1 = 80.2% of G+GE variation) with minimal crossover across sites.
- **Why it matters:** Demonstrates that bundling CA with improved stress-tolerant hybrids and cowpea rotations overcomes the transition-period penalty that limits farmer adoption; GGE stability supports a single recommendation domain for PAN53/PGS63 across southern and northern Zimbabwe, simplifying extension advice.
- **Caveats:** Four communities in two districts, all on sandy soils (>80% sand, <1% organic C); results most applicable to similar semi-arid environments. Njovo site has only 3 years of data. No economic analysis of labor or input costs provided.
