# The critical role of iron redox cycling in organic carbon dynamics of earthworm casts

**Authors:** Zheng Ni, Bin Jia, Xiangwei Wang et al.
**Venue:** Geoderma · **Date:** 2026-04-24 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.geoderma.2026.117827
**Score:** 9/10
**Source:** full text

## TL;DR

Fe(II) in fresh earthworm casts drives both rapid SOC mineralization via hydroxyl radicals (26.9% of CO₂ efflux) and long-term stabilization by co-precipitation with short-range ordered iron oxides.

## Summary

- **Problem:** Earthworm casts are known SOC hotspots attributed mainly to microbial activity; the abiotic role of Fe(II)/Fe(III) redox cycling in cast SOC dynamics was unresolved.
- **Method:** 90-day controlled incubation of fresh earthworm casts and adjacent bulk soil (Aridisol, NW China); reactive Fe(II) quantification; ROS quenching experiments (TBA scavenger, Fenton reagent); sequential Fe-phase fractionation (Fe_p, Fe_o, Fe_d); FT-ICR-MS on Fe_o-associated OC; substrate-explicit thermodynamic modelling.
- **Key result:** Fresh casts had 5.53× higher reactive Fe(II) than bulk soil; •OH was 3.34× higher. ROS quenching (TBA) reduced day-0 CO₂ emissions 86.2%, confirming •OH as responsible for 26.9% of initial SOC mineralization. Over 90 days, Fe(II) oxidation increased short-range ordered iron (Fe_o-OC from 904 to 1295 mg kg⁻¹) and shifted OM stabilization from adsorption (low OC/Fe ratios ~0.9–1.2) to co-precipitation (OC/Fe up to 2.9). FT-ICR-MS showed Fe_o-OC in aged casts enriched in lipid/protein- and aminosugar-like compounds (thermodynamically labile but physically shielded), with average molecular weight rising from 394 to 450 Da and NOSC becoming less negative (−1.245 → −0.648).
- **Why it matters:** Identifies a previously overlooked abiotic-biotic coupling: the same Fe redox chemistry simultaneously accelerates early SOC loss and drives longer-term mineral-protected OC accumulation in earthworm casts; relevant to soils consuming 2–15 Mg ha⁻¹ yr⁻¹ of organic residues in agroecosystems.
- **Caveats:** Single site (Aridisol, dryland China); *Metaphire guillelmi* only — generalizability across earthworm species and soil mineralogy uncertain; 90-day incubation may not capture full stabilization trajectory; ROS contributions quantified via proxies (fluorescent probes, quenching agents).
