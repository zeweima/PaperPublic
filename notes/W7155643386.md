# Ice-Dynamic Constraints on Glacier Climatic Mass Balance Using Inverse Techniques

**Authors:** Anna Zöller, Oskar Herrmann, Guillaume Jouvet et al.
**Venue:** Geophysical Research Letters · **Date:** 2026-04-25 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1029/2025gl120119
**Score:** 8/10<sup> top pick</sup>
**Source:** full text

## TL;DR
Remote-sensing-only inversion via the Instructed Glacier Model recovers glacier climatic mass balance for 17 of 25 Swiss glaciers with RMSE of 0.66 m w.e./a versus GLAMOS ground truth.

## Summary
- **Problem:** Glacier climatic mass balance (CMB) modeling requires scarce in situ meteorological and stake measurements; data-sparse high-mountain regions lack calibration data entirely.
- **Method:** Data assimilation using the Instructed Glacier Model (IGM v2.1.0) jointly optimizes ice thickness and surface velocity fields to minimize misfit against remotely sensed observations (elevation change from Hugonnet et al. 2021 over 2000–2020, ITS_LIVE/Sentinel-1 velocities, GlaThiDa thickness). Flux divergence (∇·(uh)) is then combined with surface elevation change (∂H/∂t) to derive spatially distributed CMB; glacier-wide ELA and accumulation/ablation gradients are extracted via piecewise linear regression. Validated on 25 GLAMOS glaciers (Swiss Alps), with Rhône Glacier as the primary test case plus a synthetic sensitivity experiment.
- **Key result:** For glaciers >1 km², ELA RMSE vs. GLAMOS = 98 m and CMB RMSE = 0.22 m w.e./a. Across all 25 glaciers, RMSE = 122 m (ELA) and 0.66 m w.e./a (CMB). Mean CMB across the network = −0.62 m w.e./a; highest mass loss rate on Glacier d'Otemma at −1.42 m w.e./a. Ice thickness is the dominant source of uncertainty; surface velocity coverage matters less provided observations are well distributed. Method fails reliably for glaciers <1 km² and when input datasets are temporally incoherent.
- **Why it matters:** Provides a transferable, ground-data-free pathway to estimate glacier CMB and solid precipitation in ungauged regions; directly applicable to global glacier inventories and future ContinuIX intercomparison exercises.
- **Caveats:** CMB parameters represent a ~20-year average and cannot resolve interannual variability. Method performance degrades for small, slow, thin glaciers. IGM inversion hyper-parameters require manual tuning and were optimized per-glacier here. Uncertainty from glacier outline and DEM errors was not propagated. Model-specific (IGM) inversion biases are unquantified.
