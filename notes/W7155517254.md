# Quaternary landscape evolution of Apennines peri-Adriatic belt: Insights into climate and tectonics from the fluvial record

**Authors:** Valeria Ruscitto, Michele Delchiaro, Maïlys Richard et al.
**Venue:** Science Advances · **Date:** 2026-04-24 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1126/sciadv.aec5376
**Score:** 8/10<sup> top pick</sup>
**Source:** full text

## TL;DR
Fluvial terrace chronologies from three Apennine rivers show climate-paced aggradation since ~1 Ma and spatially variable tectonic uplift rates of 0.07–0.25 mm yr⁻¹.

## Summary
- **Problem:** Separating climatic from tectonic drivers of fluvial terrace formation in the central Apennines peri-Adriatic belt has been limited by sparse and inconsistent chronological constraints across the region.
- **Method:** Semiautomatic DTM-based extraction of tread and basal strath surfaces for three rivers (Tenna, Aso, Tesino), combined with 1:10,000 geological mapping. Nine new IRSL (pIRIR₂₉₀) and radiocarbon ages were integrated with published regional chronologies; age-elevation modeling extracted uplift-rate histories back to ~1 Ma using strath surface elevations relative to modern thalweg.
- **Key result:** Cumulative probability distributions of terrace ages cluster most strongly during Marine Isotope Stage (MIS) 2 cooling/glacial phases; older terraces show more dispersed ages consistent with MIS 6, 8, and 10. Uplift-rate modeling for the Tenna River yielded 0.25 ± 0.05 mm yr⁻¹ from ~1 Ma to 386 ka, declining to 0.18 ± 0.09 mm yr⁻¹ (386–207 ka) and 0.07 ± 0.05 mm yr⁻¹ (207–40 ka). Along-strike differences among basins indicate differential crustal uplift or variable anticline growth rates rather than a uniform regional signal.
- **Why it matters:** Demonstrates that fill terrace deposits can yield reliable tectonic uplift signals when basal strath ages are constrained by climate-cycle assumptions, resolving a long-standing methodological debate. Provides the first robust multi-basin uplift-rate history for the central Apennines foreland, constraining seismic hazard models tied to blind thrust fault activity.
- **Caveats:** Absolute age uncertainties are substantial (IRSL errors 13–42 ka); assuming deposition onset at glacial cooling phase start introduces model circularity for the climatic interpretation. Middle Pleistocene basins were smaller and less mature, potentially confounding terrace genesis with drainage network evolution. Only three basins sampled along a ~100 km strike length.
