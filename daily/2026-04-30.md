### Hydrology & water resources

**[Catchment precipitation partitioning in the Budyko framework is controlled by root zone storage capacity](https://doi.org/10.1088/1748-9326/ae612a)** — Ibrahim et al. · *Environmental Research Letters* · score 10 · [notes](../notes/W7154954214.md)
Root zone storage capacity (S_r,max) is the dominant control on Budyko ω-parameter deviations across >5,000 global catchments (Spearman ρ = 0.68 globally; 0.87 in cold, 0.83 in Mediterranean climates), outperforming all other tested controls including climate aridity. Establishes S_r,max as a physically grounded parameterization target for LSMs and links vegetation rooting depth adaptation to catchment-scale ET/runoff partitioning.

**[Underestimated agricultural losses due to flooding](https://doi.org/10.1126/sciadv.aed2754)** — Zhang et al. · *Science Advances* · score 9 · [notes](../notes/W7154479299.md)
A flood-stress algorithm coupling CaMa-Flood floodplain hydrodynamics with GGCMI3 crop models corrects 21–29% yield biases under extreme wet conditions. Under SSP585, annual US flood losses reach ~$287M (maize), $234M (soybean), $212M (wheat); flood damages surpass drought losses in 27–55% of states depending on crop. Framework is directly usable in ISIMIP-type impact assessments.

**[Rising rainfall variability amplifies hydroclimate extremes in eastern China](https://doi.org/10.1088/1748-9326/ae5fb4)** — Pei et al. · *Environmental Research Letters* · score 9 · [notes](../notes/W7154462406.md)

**[Exploring controls on solute export mechanisms in anthropogenically impacted catchments in Southern Germany](https://doi.org/10.5194/hess-30-2059-2026)** — Frietsch & Schuetz · *HESS* · score 9 · [notes](../notes/W7154453137.md)
Warmer, drier decades since 2012 (ΔT_air +1°C, ΔP −86 mm yr⁻¹) shifted nutrient c-Q dynamics from dilution toward enrichment across 40 catchments for SRP, TP, and TOC, even as mean concentrations declined. Evapotranspiration emerged as the dominant climatic control amplifying SRP/TP enrichment, with implications for peak-load exceedance of water quality standards.

**[Quantitative apportionment of multiple water sources, Changjiang Estuary](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.watres.2026.125949)** — Zhu et al. · *Water Research* · score 9 · [notes](../notes/W7154452919.md)
