## Top picks

- [ ] **[Quantifying the safe operating space for the Amazon rainforest under climate change and deforestation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27681)** · Krönke et al. · *arXiv (physics.ao-ph)* · [notes](../notes/arxiv-2604.27681.md)

  > At 1.4 °C warming and 17% deforestation — current conditions — the Amazon is already on the 20% tipping isoline, with >30% of forest area at high risk of state transition and total forest loss reaching ~37% once tipping is included. A reduced-complexity moisture-recycling network model on a 416-cell grid across SSP2-4.5/3-7.0/5-8.5 shows that combined stressors are far more dangerous than either alone: the two-dimensional safe operating space is bounded by ≤2 °C warming and ≤15% deforestation individually, but synergistic moisture-recycling feedbacks collapse this space below current conditions. Scenario-based (road-expansion) deforestation is near-worst-case because it targets moisture-critical western regions first. Halting deforestation is the more actionable lever given tipping effects saturate around 30% forest loss.

- [ ] **[PINN-Cast: Exploring the Role of Continuous-Depth NODE in Transformers and Physics Informed Loss as Soft Physical Constraints in Short-term Weather Forecasting](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27313)** · Saleem et al. · *arXiv (cs.CV)* · [notes](../notes/arxiv-2604.27313.md)

  > Replacing discrete residual updates in transformer encoder blocks with Neural ODE layers (dopri5 solver) plus kinetic energy and thermodynamic advection soft-physics penalties reduces 36 h T2m RMSE to 1.42 K vs. 1.70 K for ClimODE and 2.85 K for ClimaX on ERA5 WeatherBench at 5.625°. A two-branch attention module fuses standard patch-wise self-attention with a finite-difference derivative branch sensitive to temporal change. Ablations confirm each component — NODE residuals, dual-branch attention, physics loss — individually improves on vanilla ViT, with the full combination giving best results at all lead times 6–36 h. Training takes ~2 days on 4 V100 GPUs, substantially cheaper than operational NWP.

- [ ] **[Representative Spectral Correlation Network for Multi-source Remote Sensing Image Classification](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27323)** · Gong et al. · *arXiv (cs.CV)* · [notes](../notes/arxiv-2604.27323.md)

  > RSCNet fuses hyperspectral imagery with SAR or LiDAR by coupling band selection to fusion rather than treating them as sequential steps: structural priors from SAR/LiDAR drive Top-k sparse attention that selects only 20–50% of HSI bands before cross-source adaptive fusion. On three benchmark datasets (Augsburg HSI+SAR, Berlin HSI+SAR, Houston2013 HSI+LiDAR) RSCNet tops all 10 comparison methods — OA/Kappa of 91.50/0.878, 78.19/0.654, and 92.66/0.920 respectively — while using only 2.88 M parameters and 0.33 GFLOPs vs. 36.8 M (FusAtNet). Multi-source fusion outperforms HSI-only by up to 3.3 OA points. Directly applicable to EnMAP+Sentinel-1 and AVIRIS+LiDAR workflows.

- [ ] **[ZAYAN: Disentangled Contrastive Transformer for Tabular Remote Sensing Data](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27606)** · Habib et al. · *arXiv (cs.CV)* · [notes](../notes/arxiv-2604.27606.md)

  > Feature-level (not sample-level) contrastive SSL with a redundancy penalty (||Z⊤Z − I||²_F) forcing feature orthogonality outperforms 31 tabular deep-learning baselines on 8 remote-sensing and flood-prediction datasets, achieving average rank 1.06 ± 0.17 vs. next-best TabICL at 3.38. ZAYAN achieves best or tied-best on 7/8 datasets — 99.66% on Crop Mapping, 97.21% on Forest Type, 93.61% on Pluvial Flood (50% label noise). Ablation confirms the redundancy penalty alone accounts for ~9 pp on Urban Land Cover. Feature-level contrastive learning is the key insight enabling robustness to heterogeneous, redundant tabular features under distribution shift.

- [ ] **[Spectral Dynamic Attention Network for Hyperspectral Image Super-Resolution](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27326)** · Zhang et al. · *arXiv (cs.CV)* · [notes](../notes/arxiv-2604.27326.md)

  > SDANet (2.67 M params, 9.48 GFLOPs) uses dynamic sparse channel attention — data-driven Top-K gating that zeros redundant spectral attention entries — plus frequency-domain FFN branches (FFT + depthwise convolution, 5×5 and 3×3 kernels) to achieve best-in-class PSNR/SSIM/SAM on Chikusei (PSNR 40.51 dB at ×4) and Pavia Centre (32.24 dB at ×4) across seven baselines. Ablation validates both the dynamic gating (fixed-K alternatives reduce PSNR) and the frequency FFN. Code available at github.com/oucailab/SDANet; applicable to precision agriculture and environmental monitoring HSI workflows where spatial resolution is the bottleneck.

- [ ] **[Hyperspectral Unmixing Hierarchies](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.16969)** · Garrett et al. · *arXiv (eess.IV)* · [notes](../notes/arxiv-2604.16969.md)

  > BLUTH networks impose a hierarchical abundance sum constraint on deep NMF — a binary tree where each parent abundance equals the sum of children — and grow topology without a pre-specified endmember count via SMUG (four alternating sparsity modalities). On lab scenes with true ground-truth proportions, BLUTH-AA records top-2 IoU for every endmember; on Skagerrak ocean PACE/HYPSO-2 imagery it cleanly separates land, cloud, coccolithophore bloom, and CDOM river runoff at the first hierarchy split. Storage footprint is 10.4 kB vs. 2.3 MB for conventional methods — critical for new hyperspectral satellite constellations (EMIT, EnMAP, PACE). Training time is significant (hours to >1 day per scene) and scalability to full PACE databases remains undemonstrated.

- [ ] **[A generalised pre-training strategy for deep learning networks in semantic segmentation of remotely sensed images](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27704)** · Fang et al. · *arXiv (cs.CV)* · [notes](../notes/arxiv-2604.27704.md)

  > Channel Shuffling Pre-training (CSP) — duplicating ImageNet channels to match target modality then randomly shuffling order — forces models to learn spatial/structural rather than spectral cues, achieving SOTA on all four benchmarks (iSAID 67.4% mIoU, MFNet +6.6%, PST900 +13.83%, Potsdam 91.88% mF1) using only ImageNet-1K with ConvNeXt-T/Swin-T. Gains are largest for multi-channel or multimodal inputs (thermal, DSM, IR) and modest for 3-channel RGB. CSP-trained models are robust to channel-order perturbation, confirming reduced spectral over-fitting. Provides a path toward unified RS foundation models without domain-specific pre-training data collection.

- [ ] **[Hyperspectral Image Classification via Efficient Global Spectral Supertoken Clustering](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.isprsjprs.2026.04.041)** · Liu et al. · *arXiv (cs.CV) / ISPRS J.* · [notes](../notes/arxiv-2604.27364.md)

  > DSCC (dual-stage Spectrum-Constrained Clustering-based Classifier) computes global multi-criteria feature distance once per image — spectral bands + spectral derivative + deep semantic features — to assign pixels to clustering centers, then predicts at cluster-token level using a hybrid Transformer-Mamba. On WHU-OHS (7795 images, 24 classes), it reaches CF1 0.728 at 197.75 FPS and 34.33 G FLOPs — outperforming next-best S2Mamba (CF1 0.723) at ~2.5× fewer FLOPs. A Category Proportion-aware Soft Label handles mixed-cover tokens at cluster boundaries. Relevance for operational land-use monitoring at real-time inference speed.

- [ ] **[Towards Generalizable Mapping of Hedges and Linear Woody Features from Earth Observation Data: a national Product for Germany](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27247)** · Hoeser et al. · *arXiv (cs.CV)* · [notes](../notes/arxiv-2604.27247.md)

  > A two-module pipeline — flexible EO input interface (BKG DOP20 20 cm orthophotos, PlanetScope CHM at 3 m, Maxar CHMv2 at 0.73 m) converting heterogeneous data to binary woody-vegetation masks, plus a U-Net segmentation model trained exclusively on synthetic masks — maps hedgerows and linear woody features nationally across Germany from three sensor types using one model without retraining. Validated against biotope-mapping reference data from four federal states. Decoupling vegetation-mask creation from linear-feature extraction enables plug-and-play data source replacement — critical for EU Nature Restoration Regulation and CAP compliance monitoring at national scale. Synthetic training removes the label-scarcity bottleneck for cross-country transfer.

- [ ] **[HQ-UNet: A Hybrid Quantum-Classical U-Net with a Quantum Bottleneck for Remote Sensing Image Segmentation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27206)** · Hossain et al. · *arXiv (cs.CV)* · [notes](../notes/arxiv-2604.27206.md)

  > Replacing the classical U-Net bottleneck with a 16-qubit quantum convolutional circuit (RX/RY/RZ encoding, 2D separable quanvolution) raises mIoU from 0.6451 to 0.8050 (+0.1599) and OA from 82.43% to 94.76% (+12.33%) on LandCover.ai vs. the classical U-Net, while beating the best prior hybrid quantum model (FQCNN, mIoU 0.2000) by 0.6050. The quantum circuit runs on a noiseless simulator; NISQ hardware noise is untested and the LandCover.ai evaluation is single-dataset. Conceptually relevant as quantum hardware matures for parameter-efficient Earth observation models.
