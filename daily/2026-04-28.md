## Top picks

- [ ] **Amazon forest loss: An all-sky biophysical top-of-atmosphere cooling feedback** — Science, Dror et al. Cloud-driven albedo increases from Amazon deforestation produce shortwave TOA cooling of up to 6.8 ± 0.6 W m⁻² in high-loss areas, doubling the surface-brightening effect alone and suggesting current models underestimate this compensating mechanism. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1126/science.adz8296) · [notes](../notes/W7155360464.md)

- [ ] **Human footprint on estuarine tidal hydrodynamics** — Nature Geoscience, Beemster et al. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41561-026-01969-4) · [notes](papers/notes/W7155510768.md)

- [ ] **From Noisy Historical Maps to Time-Series Oil Palm Mapping Without Annotation in Malaysia and Indonesia (2020–2024)** — arXiv (cs.CV), Kuapanich et al. A U-Net trained with DMI loss on coarse 100 m historical labels produces the first publicly available 10 m annual oil palm plantation maps for Indonesia and Malaysia (2020–2024), revealing a coverage peak in 2022 and ongoing encroachment into flooded wetland areas. [DOI](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.23776) · [notes](papers/notes/arxiv-2604.23776.md)

- [ ] **Physiological process-based feature engineering enables robust estimation of crop gross primary production under data-limited conditions** — Agricultural and Forest Meteorology, Horikoshi et al. A hybrid ANN (ANNh) that first converts environmental variables into leaf-level photosynthetic rates via process models stays robust under single-site training (R² 0.78 vs. −0.26 for a conventional ANN) and under extreme drought where the conventional ANN produces physically implausible outputs. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.agrformet.2026.111191) · [notes](papers/notes/W7155375573.md)

- [ ] **A novel memory-based deep learning framework for reliable joint estimation of daily vegetation carbon fluxes** — Agricultural and Forest Meteorology, Chen et al. DemLSTM — a dual-encoder multi-task LSTM jointly estimating GPP, RECO, and NEE — reduces carbon closure bias from ~0.41 g C m⁻² d⁻¹ (RF/XGBoost) to 0.025 g C m⁻² d⁻¹ while implicitly capturing carbon–water coupling (transpiration probe r = 0.89). [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.agrformet.2026.111198) · [notes](papers/notes/W7155380021.md)

- [ ] **The critical role of iron redox cycling in organic carbon dynamics of earthworm casts** — Geoderma, Ni et al. Reactive Fe(II) in fresh earthworm casts is 5.53× bulk soil, driving hydroxyl-radical-mediated SOC mineralization (26.9% of CO₂ flux) while Fe oxidation to short-range ordered oxides simultaneously promotes long-term OM stabilization via co-precipitation — a dual abiotic role models currently ignore. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.geoderma.2026.117827) · [notes](papers/notes/W7155527983.md)

- [ ] **Dynamic Parameterization of Priestley-Taylor Coefficient for a More Accurate Potential Evapotranspiration Estimation in Ungauged Regions** — Environmental Research Letters, Raghav & Kumar. Biome-specific, dynamically estimated Priestley-Taylor alpha (driven by temperature and radiation) derived from 3,128 site-years across 246 FluxNet sites outperforms the fixed α = 1.26 for PET, actual ET, and plant stress, with drop-in functional equations ready for land-surface models. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1088/1748-9326/ae6464) · [notes](papers/notes/W7155531902.md)

- [ ] **Technical note: High Nash–Sutcliffe Efficiencies conceal poor simulations of interannual variance in seasonal regimes** — HESS, Ruzzante et al. Across 18 regional and global hydrologic models, high NSE/KGE in strongly seasonal catchments masks systematically poor interannual variance simulation (lower NSE_interannual in 17 of 18 models), threatening climate-change projections precisely in tropical, alpine, and polar regions most vulnerable to non-stationarity. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.5194/hess-30-2337-2026) · [notes](papers/notes/W7155360582.md)

- [ ] **Seasonal dynamics of closed shallow lakes nutrient status controlled by lacustrine groundwater discharge** — HESS, Sun et al.
