## Top picks

- [ ] **Methane leakage thresholds for net climate benefits of wastewater biogas recovery** — Nature Sustainability, Li et al. Observed leakage rates of 0.4–65% across 50+ US wastewater facilities mean a large fraction already exceed net-zero thresholds; technology- and grid-specific safe limits span 2–10% of biogas produced, and grid decarbonization shifts the optimal pathway from CHP to renewable natural gas. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41893-026-01818-7) · [notes](../notes/W7156218028.md)

- [ ] **First global carbon dynamics from an observational and process-informed hybrid perspective** — Agricultural and Forest Meteorology, Zhu et al. A process-informed ML hybrid (UFLUX) pins global net land carbon uptake at 16 PgC yr⁻¹ — between process-model (6) and pure-upscaling (26) estimates — tracing the divergence to respiration parameterisation, not GPP; tropical photosynthesis gains are nearly offset by rising respiration, flagging tipping-point risk. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.agrformet.2026.111197) · [notes](../notes/W7155976704.md)

- [ ] **Mapping PFAS Exceedance Risk in China's Surface Water: A Machine Learning Approach Informed by Source Distribution** — Environmental Science & Technology, Wáng et al. A Geographically Weighted Random Forest model (accuracy 0.83, ROC-AUC 0.91) produces a 1 km resolution PFAS exceedance-risk map for China, placing 80–90 million people in high-risk zones concentrated in the eastern coastal plain and key inland industrial provinces. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1021/acs.est.6c00574) · [notes](../notes/W7156054837.md)

- [ ] **Bidirectional feedbacks between Abies koreana dieback and soil properties slow soil carbon turnover in a subalpine forest** — Biogeochemistry, Koh et al. Korean fir mortality on nutrient-poor sandy slopes suppresses rhizosphere priming and disrupts litter–soil connectivity, producing older SOC and higher C:N at high-mortality sites — a bidirectional feedback loop that constrains SOM turnover predictions under globally expanding conifer dieback. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1007/s10533-026-01332-0) · [notes](../notes/W7155710648.md)

- [ ] **Temperature sensitivity of labile and stabilised organic carbon decomposition in different land uses** — Biogeochemistry, Filep et al. Q₁₀ values (2.96–3.71) do not differ between labile and stabilised soil C fractions, refuting kinetic theory; OM chemical composition (aryl C, carbonyl C) governs temperature sensitivity, and cultivated aggregate fractions show anomalously high Q₁₀ = 5.38, highlighting warming vulnerability of aggregate-bound C in arable soils. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1007/s10533-026-01338-8) · [notes](../notes/W7156099716.md)

- [ ] **Holocene Nile dynamics shaped the physical and cultural landscape of ancient Nubia** — PNAS, Peeters et al. 26 OSL- and ¹⁴C-dated sediment cores show Early–Middle Holocene valley incision followed by floodplain aggradation at ~4,000 y BP; cataract-constrained Late Holocene stability then sustained fertile agricultural land that underpinned the rise of ancient Kush at Napata (~1070 BCE–350 CE). [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.2529986123) · [notes](../notes/W7155897983.md)

- [ ] **The PhenoMeNals framework for grapevine yield and quality prediction** — Agricultural and Forest Meteorology, Bregaglio & Bajocco. An open-source R framework coupling a phenology model with eight ecophysiological cross-season memory signals achieves LOOCV R² >0.85 for 20 of 28 site × variety combinations across three continents, without site-specific recalibration. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.agrformet.2026.111202) · [notes](../notes/W7155679546.md)

- [ ] **Winter North Atlantic tripole sea surface temperature drives following summer synchronous heat waves over Eurasia since the 2000s** — Environmental Research Letters, Chen et al. Winter North Atlantic tripole SST anomalies persist into summer via air-sea interaction and trigger an eastward atmospheric wave train producing simultaneous anticyclonic heat-wave conditions over Eastern Europe and East Asia, a teleconnection whose persistence has strengthened since 2000. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1088/1748-9326/ae6520) · [notes](../notes/W7155786486.md)

- [ ] **Spatio-temporal patterns of wheat yield losses and environmental stresses in Europe** — Environmental Research Letters, Collins et al. Crop model simulations at 25 km resolution (1985–2014) attribute 45% of simulated European wheat production to environmental stress losses, with 59% of that driven by water shortage; losses declined 0.22%/yr overall as severe terminal drought environments contracted in southern Europe. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1088/1748-9326/ae651e) · [notes](../notes/W7155892272.md)

- [ ] **Repeated snowball–hothouse cycles within the Neoproterozoic Sturtian glaciation** — PNAS, Minsky et al. Franklin igneous province weathering drives a coupled climate–carbon-cycle box model into a limit cycle alternating between Snowball and hothouse states, providing the first self-consistent mechanism for the anomalously long (~56 Myr) Sturtian glaciation and predicting stable syn-Sturtian atmospheric oxygen. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.2525919123) · [notes](../notes/W7155981520.md)

- [ ] **A Geographically Weighted Gaussian Process Regression (GW-GPR) emulator of anthropogenic PM2.5 from GEOS-Chem High Performance** — Geoscientific Model Development, Wong et al. GW-GPR emulates GCHP global surface PM2.5 at grid-cell scale with R²=0.94–0.99 across four policy scenarios in <10 CPU seconds versus ~3000 CPU hours for GCHP, enabling air quality scenario screening in integrated assessment without atmospheric computing infrastructure. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.5194/gmd-19-3335-2026) · [notes](../notes/W7156169852.md)
