## Top picks (10 fully-summarized of 92)

### 1. High-resolution land surface modeling of climate and CO2 effects on ecosystem carbon-water coupling across the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau
*Agricultural and Forest Meteorology, Xi et al.*  ·  [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.agrformet.2026.111195)  ·  Score 10/10
High-resolution LSM partitions climate vs. CO2 forcings on coupled C-water fluxes over the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau. Refines understanding of alpine ecosystem responses to global change in a hydrologically sensitive headwater region.

### 2. Physiological process-based feature engineering enables robust estimation of crop gross primary production under data-limited conditions
*Agricultural and Forest Meteorology, Horikoshi et al.*  ·  [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.agrformet.2026.111191)  ·  Score 10/10
Hybrid ANNh injects C3/C4 photosynthesis, energy balance, and stomatal conductance outputs (plus EVI) into a neural net for cropland GPP. Under data-limited cross-site validation and extreme drought, ANNh stays robust while a vanilla ANN degrades — physiology acts as regularization.

### 3. Hidden Complexity in Reservoir Flow Regulation Revealed by Spectral Analysis
*Geophysical Research Letters, Zhang et al.*  ·  [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1029/2026gl121654)  ·  Score 10/10
Spectral decomposition of inflow-outflow at 205 US reservoirs identifies four operational modes (high-frequency attenuation, seasonal amplification, seasonal smoothing, high-frequency amplification) that better describe regulation behavior than design-purpose classifications. Larger reservoirs regulate more strongly in their characteristic mode.

### 4. The Value of Forecasters-in-the-Loop in Real-Time Flood Forecasting in the Age of Machine Learning
*Geophysical Research Letters, Tran et al.*  ·  [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1029/2025gl118317)  ·  Score 10/10
First head-to-head test of ML hydrology against an actual operational system (CNRFC + CHPS + human forecasters). Forecasters-in-the-loop beat ML out to 96 hr lead, even when ML uses observed forcings and CNRFC uses biased weather forecasts; the human-guided system also degrades more gracefully.

### 5. Technical note: High Nash-Sutcliffe Efficiencies conceal poor simulations of interannual variance in seasonal regimes
*HESS, Ruzzante et al.*  ·  [DOI](https://doi.org/10.5194/hess-30-2337-2026)  ·  Score 10/10
Across 18 regional/global hydrologic models, NSE/KGE peak in highly seasonal catchments precisely where the interannual-variance component of NSE is worst — meaning tropical, alpine, and polar regions, the most climate-vulnerable, are likely served worst by these models for long-term projections.

### 6. Sustainable global irrigation expansion could support only two-thirds of croplands under 1.5 °C and 3 °C warming
*Nature Food, He & Rosa*  ·  [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1038/s43016-026-01338-9)  ·  Score 9/10
Sustainable blue-water expansion can supply only ~2/3 of global croplands under both 1.5 °C and 3 °C warming. A binding water constraint on adaptation: food security cannot lean on irrigation alone.

### 7. Amazon forest loss: An all-sky biophysical top-of-atmosphere cooling feedback
*Science, Dror & Feingold*  ·  [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1126/science.adz8296)  ·  Score 9/10
Two decades of multi-source satellite obs: TOA shortwave + longwave cooling scales with forest loss; high-loss areas reach 6.8 ± 0.6 W/m² shortwave cooling, with cloud-driven albedo gain roughly doubling the surface-brightening alone. Cloud responses must be in climate models and land-management policy.

### 8. ArchesWeatherGen: Skillful and compute-efficient probabilistic weather forecasting with machine learning
*Science Advances, Couairon et al.*  ·  [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1126/sciadv.adx2372)  ·  Score 9/10
Flow-matching diffusion model that maps deterministic ML forecasts onto the ERA5 distribution. Beats IFS ENS and NeuralGCM on all WeatherBench headline variables (except NeuralGCM geopotential) at lower compute — democratizes generative ML for ensemble weather.

### 9. China's lake expansion amplified rapid CO2 emissions
*Science Advances, Feng et al.*  ·  [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1126/sciadv.aea4657)  ·  Score 9/10
Compiled lake-CO2 flux data + ML + lake-area data: emissions rose from 11.25 → 13.94 Tg C/yr (2000–2021) with an abrupt post-2010 jump tied to lake expansion (71–462 km²/yr). Large lakes (>50 km²) account for ~62%; lakes offset ~12% of national wetland C sinks.

### 10. Network Divergence Reveals Predictable Pathways of Extreme Rainfall in Central India
*Geophysical Research Letters, Raghuvanshi & Agarwal*  ·  [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1029/2026gl121973)  ·  Score 8/10
Directed event-synchronization networks predict >60% of >95th-percentile Central India rainfall extremes from preceding east-coast rainfall + low-pressure anomaly + moisture convergence. Cheap, interpretable alternative to NWP for early warning.
