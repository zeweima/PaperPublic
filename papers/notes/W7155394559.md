# Long-term organic manure substitution enhances soil carbon stability via organically complexed iron oxides: Evidence from a 12-year field experiment

**Authors:** Yiming Xian, Xingyu Li, Rong Huang et al.
**Venue:** Geoderma · **Date:** 2026-04-23 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.geoderma.2026.117825
**Score:** 9/10<sup> top pick</sup>
**Source:** full text

## TL;DR
Full replacement of synthetic N with organic manure maximizes mineral-associated carbon stabilization via Fe co-precipitation without increasing greenhouse gas emissions.

## Summary
- **Problem:** Optimal organic manure substitution ratios for long-term soil carbon stabilization are unclear, and fraction-specific responses of mineral-associated organic matter (MAOM) to iron oxide transformations under varying substitution levels are poorly quantified.
- **Method:** 12-year rice–wheat rotation field experiment (Sichuan, China) with four treatments: 0% (CF), 25% (OS25), 50% (OS50), and 100% (OS100) organic N substitution of synthetic fertilizer. Soil density fractionation isolated MAOM; sequential selective dissolution (pyrophosphate, hydroxylamine-HCl, dithionite-HCl) partitioned Fe₍PP₎ (organically complexed), Fe₍HH₎ (amorphous), and Fe₍DH₎ (crystalline) oxides and their associated OC. Linear mixed-effects models and automated linear modeling identified predictors of MAOC/SOC ratio.
- **Key result:** OS100 increased SOC by 11.23% and MAOC by 23.73% relative to CF. Organically complexed iron oxides (Fe₍PP₎) became the dominant iron phase in MAOM, elevated 56.60–73.57% above CF at OS100. Fe₍PP₎-associated OC (OC₍PP₎) showed a non-linear response: declining at OS25–OS50, then rising 13.53–35.22% above other treatments at OS100. OC/Fe molar ratios for Fe₍PP₎ ranged 14.46–15.21 (bulk soil) and 20.00–22.74 (MAOM), well above the co-precipitation threshold of 6, indicating co-precipitation as the dominant stabilization mechanism. Total greenhouse gas emissions (CO₂ + CH₄) showed no significant difference among treatments despite the OS50 treatment having highest cumulative emissions.
- **Why it matters:** Establishes that 100% organic manure substitution activates Fe₍PP₎-mediated co-precipitation in MAOM, enabling maximal carbon stabilization without a GHG emissions penalty — directly informing organic amendment strategies for long-term carbon sequestration in paddy systems. Fe₍PP₎-related variables were the dominant predictors of MAOC/SOC ratio in MAOM (cumulative importance 0.74), outranking crystalline Fe variables that dominate bulk soil predictions.
- **Caveats:** Single site (Fe-rich paddy soil, Southwest China); redox-driven poorly crystalline Fe formation is inherent to flooded paddy management and results may not transfer to well-aerated upland soils. POC was estimated indirectly (SOC − MAOC) rather than by direct physical fractionation. Threshold Fe₍PP₎ levels and stability of co-precipitated complexes under extreme hydrological events remain unquantified.
