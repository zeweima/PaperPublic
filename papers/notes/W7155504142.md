# Seasonal dynamics of closed shallow lakes nutrient status controlled by lacustrine groundwater discharge

**Authors:** Xiaoliang Sun, Yao Du, Hao Tian et al.
**Venue:** HESS · **Date:** 2026-04-24 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.5194/hess-30-2357-2026
**Score:** 9/10<sup> top pick</sup>
**Source:** full text

## TL;DR
In a Yangtze Basin oxbow lake, monthly net precipitation drives seasonal lacustrine groundwater discharge (35–52 mm/d), which controls lake N/P ratios and chlorophyll-a dynamics.

## Summary
- **Problem:** Intermonthly variability in lacustrine groundwater discharge (LGD) and its role in regulating lake nutrient status and eutrophication in closed shallow lakes was poorly understood, largely because prior studies relied on low-frequency (seasonal) sampling.
- **Method:** Bi-monthly high-frequency monitoring over a full hydrological year (June 2022–July 2023) at Heiwawu Oxbow Lake, central Yangtze Basin; 241 samples (lake water, groundwater wells, pore water). ²²²Rn mass balance model used to quantify LGD rates; TN, TP, Chl-a, water-level, and meteorological data collected concurrently.
- **Key result:** LGD rates ranged 35.36–51.71 mm d⁻¹ during the active groundwater discharge period (August 2022–April 2023), peaking in December 2022. LGD-TN loads ranged 10.76–24.00 mmol m⁻² d⁻¹ and LGD-TP loads 0.29–0.92 mmol m⁻² d⁻¹, both 1–2 orders of magnitude higher than atmospheric deposition and sediment diffusion inputs. LGD-TN load correlated with lake TN concentration (R² = 0.79, P < 0.05); LGD-TP with lake TP concentration (R² = 0.89, P < 0.05). The LGD-driven TN/TP molar ratio in lake water correlated with Chl-a (R² = 0.87, P < 0.05); when TN/TP approached the Redfield ratio of 16, Chl-a peaked at 98.69 µg L⁻¹. Net precipitation (precipitation minus evaporation, PME) explained 86% of variance in LGD rates via hydraulic gradient regulation (non-linear fit R² = 0.86, P < 0.05).
- **Why it matters:** Demonstrates for the first time that groundwater-driven nutrient loading modulates lake trophic status at intermonthly scales, shifting the mechanistic focus from absolute N or P concentrations to the LGD-mediated TN/TP ratio as the proximate control on phytoplankton growth. High-frequency monitoring captures this signal; quarterly campaigns would miss it. Findings are broadly transferable to the large global inventory of closed shallow lakes lacking perennial surface inflows.
- **Caveats:** Single lake, single anomalous drought year (2022 drought caused earlier hydrological isolation). Confined aquifer hydrogeology and monsoon climate may limit direct transferability. ²²²Rn end-member CV ~18–26% introduces flux uncertainty. TP dynamics partly controlled by internal adsorption/redox processes not fully resolved.
