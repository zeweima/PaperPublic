# The PhenoMeNals framework for grapevine yield and quality prediction

**Authors:** Simone Bregaglio, Sofia Bajocco
**Venue:** Agricultural and Forest Meteorology · **Date:** 2026-04-27 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.agrformet.2026.111202
**Score:** 8/10 top pick
**Source:** full text

## TL;DR

Open-source R framework integrating phenology-aware ecophysiological memory signals predicts grapevine yield and quality across sites and varieties with LOOCV R² consistently >0.85.

## Summary

- **Problem:** Most grapevine yield/quality forecasting models are site- or variety-specific and ignore carry-over effects across the two-year reproductive cycle, limiting generalizability across cultivars and climates.
- **Method:** PhenoMeNals couples a simplex-optimized chill-forcing phenology model (calibrated to BBCH observations; RMSE 6–9 days) with eight ecophysiological response functions (temperature, cold, heat, light, VPD, drought, wind, disease favorability). Each function is computed hourly, binned along a standardized 0–400 phenological timeline spanning two consecutive seasons, sigmoid-normalized to interannual percentiles, weighted by Pearson correlation with the target trait, and cumulatively summed to form "memory signals." Stepwise AIC selection and LOOCV multiple linear regression produce final predictions. Validated on 6 sites across Europe, Australia, and North America; 28 site × variety combinations; time series 12–47 years.
- **Key result:** At phenological bin 400 (harvest), LOOCV R² exceeded 0.85 for 20 of 28 site × variety combinations and exceeded 0.90 for 11 of 28; best case R² = 0.98 full / 0.95 LOOCV. Phenology simulation RMSE: 6.8 days (bud burst) to 8.7 days (maturity). Sugar/Brix predictions more variable (weakest LOOCV R² = 0.72).
- **Why it matters:** Accounts for cross-season carry-over weather effects — a key gap for perennial crops — within an open-source R package portable across cultivars and geographies without site-specific recalibration. Phase-specific predictor importance maps enable physiological interpretation.
- **Caveats:** Non-site-specific gridded weather data introduces additional uncertainty at some sites. Sugar predictions less robust than yield. Framework not yet tested outside viticulture.
