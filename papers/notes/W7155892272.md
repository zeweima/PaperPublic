# Spatio-temporal patterns of wheat yield losses and environmental stresses in Europe

**Authors:** Brian Collins, Rogerio de S. Nóia Júnior, Heidi Webber et al.
**Venue:** Environmental Research Letters · **Date:** 2026-04-27 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1088/1748-9326/ae651e
**Score:** 8/10 top pick
**Source:** abstract

## TL;DR

Water shortage drives 59% of European wheat yield losses, which averaged 111.4 Mt/yr (45% of potential) but declined 0.22%/yr from 1985–2014.

## Summary

- **Problem:** No pan-European quantitative attribution of wheat yield losses to water, nitrogen, and temperature stresses existed, nor characterization of how the spatial distribution of stress environments has shifted over recent decades.
- **Method:** Crop model simulations at 25×25 km resolution across Europe, 1985–2014, disaggregating above-ground biomass loss into water, nitrogen, and temperature limitation components; five environment types classified by daily water-deficit patterns.
- **Key result:** Environmental stresses caused 111.4 Mt/yr average production loss (45% of simulated potential); 59% attributable to water shortage. Losses declined 0.22% per annum overall. Frequency of drought-free or mild-water-stress environments increased; severe terminal drought environments decreased, especially in Italy, Romania, and Spain.
- **Why it matters:** Quantifies the dominant role of water deficit for European wheat and provides a spatially explicit stress-environment typology useful for breeding trial network design and genotype×environment studies.
- **Caveats:** Simulation-based (observed yield validation not emphasized); 25 km resolution may miss sub-field variability; period ends 2014, so recent warming and drought trends are not captured.
