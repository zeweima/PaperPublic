# Physiological process-based feature engineering enables robust estimation of crop gross primary production under data-limited conditions

**Authors:** Mizuki Horikoshi, Gaku Yokoyama, Shigehiro Kubota et al.
**Venue:** Agricultural and Forest Meteorology · **Date:** 2026-04-23 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.agrformet.2026.111191
**Score:** 9/10<sup> top pick</sup>
**Source:** full text

## TL;DR
A hybrid ANN that converts environmental variables into leaf-level photosynthetic rates via process models (ANNh) maintains robust crop GPP estimation under data-limited and extreme drought conditions where conventional ANNs collapse.

## Summary
- **Problem:** ML-based GPP models require large, diverse training datasets and degrade sharply when extrapolated to novel climates — a critical limitation as extreme weather events become more frequent.
- **Method:** ANNh pre-processes six environmental inputs (PPFD, T_a, VPD, W_s, C_a, ψ_soil) through C3/C4 photosynthesis biochemical models, energy balance, and stomatal conductance models to produce leaf-level photosynthetic rate A; A plus EVI then feed a 3-layer ANN. Tested against a conventional ANN (ANNs) and two LUE baselines (EC-LUE, MODIS) using 5-fold cross-validation and single-site training validation (SSTV) across 8 maize and 6 wheat FLUXNET2015/ICOS sites.
- **Key result:** Under 5-fold CV, ANNh matched or exceeded ANNs (maize R² 0.89 vs. 0.91, RMSE 5.09 vs. 4.49 µmol m⁻² s⁻¹). Under SSTV (data-limited), ANNs degraded severely (maize mean R² −0.26, RMSE 13.8 µmol m⁻² s⁻¹) while ANNh stayed robust (R² 0.78, RMSE 6.87 µmol m⁻² s⁻¹), matching or beating EC-LUE and MODIS baselines. Under the 2005 US-ARM drought test, ANNs produced physically implausible GPP estimates (R² −301, RMSE 53.6 µmol m⁻² s⁻¹) whereas ANNh remained stable (R² 0.36, RMSE 1.78 µmol m⁻² s⁻¹).
- **Why it matters:** Process-based feature engineering acts as physiologically grounded regularization — constraining the ML input space to physically valid variables (temperature stress, stomatal closure) suppresses overfitting more effectively than L2 regularization or dropout alone, enabling large-scale GPP assessment with sparse site data under increasingly extreme climates.
- **Caveats:** ANNh relies on only two input channels (A and EVI), limiting canopy complexity representation; EVI uncertainty from atmospheric contamination propagates into both models; process model parameters (V_cmax, J_max) use literature values that vary by cultivar and fertilization; the model occasionally outputs physiologically implausible GPP when EVI and A fall outside the training range.
