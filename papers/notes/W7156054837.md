# Mapping PFAS Exceedance Risk in China's Surface Water: A Machine Learning Approach Informed by Source Distribution

**Authors:** Yì Wáng, Shuai Shao, Qiufeng Gao et al.
**Venue:** Environmental Science & Technology · **Date:** 2026-04-27 · **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1021/acs.est.6c00574
**Score:** 9/10 top pick
**Source:** abstract

## TL;DR

A Geographically Weighted Random Forest model maps PFAS exceedance risk at 1 km resolution across China, finding 80–90 million people in high-risk zones concentrated in eastern coastal and key inland industrial areas.

## Summary

- **Problem:** Nationwide PFAS distribution in China's surface water is unknown due to sparse monitoring data, despite China being the world's leading fluorochemical producer and consumer.
- **Method:** Geographically Weighted Random Forest (GWR-RF) integrating a spatially explicit inventory of >280,000 potential PFAS point sources; trained to predict exceedance risk at 1 km resolution across China.
- **Key result:** Model accuracy = 0.83, ROC-AUC = 0.91; high-risk hotspots cluster in eastern coastal plains and key inland industrial provinces; 80–90 million people reside in high-risk areas; proximity to known PFAS users and annual precipitation are the dominant risk predictors.
- **Why it matters:** First national-scale, high-resolution PFAS risk map for China; directly supports prioritization of monitoring networks, regulatory enforcement, and targeted remediation for a population-scale contamination problem.
- **Caveats:** Risk map is based on exceedance probability, not measured concentrations; source inventory completeness uncertain; model trained on sparse observations may underestimate risk in unmonitored regions.
